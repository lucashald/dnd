Background & Origins

The Younger Evanara

Born on Evermeet, the sacred island homeland of the elves, Iawynn came to Silverymoon as a young elf when her older sister Tyrael brought the family to the Silver Marches. At approximately 120 years old, she is young by elven standards—yet already a proven warrior, a devoted paladin, and a veteran of war.

Star Elf Heritage

The Mithral Elves

Star elves, also known as mithral elves, are among the rarest of elvenkind:

  • Origin: Evermeet, the mystical isle hidden from mortal charts
  • Appearance: Luminous pale skin, silver or white hair, eyes that reflect moonlight and starlight
  • Nature: Contemplative, mystical, deeply connected to celestial phenomena
  • Rarity: Few star elves leave Evermeet; encountering one on the mainland is exceptional
  • Connection to Selûne: Many star elves feel a natural kinship with the moon goddess

Life in Silverymoon: The City of Silver provided an ideal home for the displaced Evanara sisters. Its magical academies, libraries, and temples offered both Tyrael and Iawynn opportunities to develop their respective paths—psionics and divine service.

Selûne & The Order of the Silver Ladies

Oath of the Moonmaiden

Iawynn is a paladin sworn to Selûne, the Moonmaiden, goddess of the moon, stars, navigation, wanderers, and those who seek light in darkness. She serves in the Order of the Silver Ladies, an elite order of Selunite paladins.

Selûne's Teachings

  • Light in Darkness: Be a beacon of hope to those lost in shadow
  • Protection of Travelers: Defend wanderers, sailors, and all who journey
  • Opposition to Shar: Eternal enemy of Shar, Selûne's dark twin sister
  • Change & Transformation: Embrace the waxing and waning of life
  • Guidance: Help others find their path, both literal and spiritual
  • Compassion: Show mercy to the lost, but strike down those who spread darkness

The Order of the Silver Ladies

Elite Selunite Paladins

The Order of the Silver Ladies is a prestigious and sacred order within Selûne's faith:

Requirements:

  • Unwavering devotion to Selûne
  • Proven combat ability and holy power
  • Commitment to defending the innocent and opposing darkness
  • Willingness to serve as both warrior and spiritual leader

Duties:

  • Defend the Faithful: Protect Selunite temples, clergy, and worshipers
  • Hunt the Darkness: Seek out and destroy servants of Shar and other evil
  • Guide the Lost: Rescue those trapped in literal or spiritual darkness
  • Oppose Lycanthropy: Cure or contain those afflicted with the curse (while protecting innocent victims)
  • Champion the Light: Serve as visible, public defenders of good

Recognition: Silver Ladies are known throughout Faerûn as formidable holy warriors. They wear distinctive armor blessed by Selunite clergy, often adorned with moon symbols and silver inlay.

The War of the Silver Marches

Baptism by Battle

When orc hordes descended upon the Silver Marches in recent years, Iawynn answered the call without hesitation. As a Silver Lady of Selûne, she fought on the front lines, her armor gleaming in moonlight, her sword blazing with holy fire.

Iawynn's Role in the War

  • Frontline Warrior: Led charges against orc formations
  • Beacon of Hope: Her radiant presence inspired allied troops
  • Holy Healer: Used Selûne's power to heal the wounded between battles
  • Defender of Innocents: Evacuated civilians and protected refugees
  • Destroyer of Evil: Hunted orc shamans and dark cultists

Fighting Alongside Tyrael

While Iawynn fought openly in silver armor, her sister Tyrael operated in the shadows—eliminating orc leaders with psychic blades, gathering intelligence, sabotaging supply lines. The sisters complemented each other perfectly:

Iawynn (The Light) Tyrael (The Shadow)
Fought in the open Worked in secrecy
Inspired the army Eliminated key threats
Healed the wounded Prevented future casualties
Symbol of hope Instrument of strategy

War's Legacy

  • Combat Experience: Hardened by brutal warfare
  • Recognition: Known and respected throughout the Silver Marches
  • Loss: Saw comrades fall; carries the weight of their sacrifice
  • Purpose: Renewed commitment to defending the innocent
  • Sisterly Bond: War deepened trust and reliance on Tyrael

Paladin Powers & Abilities

Gifts of the Moonmaiden

As a paladin of Selûne, Iawynn channels divine power granted by her goddess:

Divine Smite

When her blade strikes evil, it erupts with moonlight—radiant energy that burns the wicked and illuminates darkness. Against fiends and undead, the power is especially devastating.

Lay on Hands

Selûne's healing touch flows through Iawynn's hands. She can cure wounds, diseases, and even neutralize poisons with a prayer and the Moonmaiden's grace.

Aura of Protection

Those who stand near Iawynn feel Selûne's protective presence—a shield of faith that wards against harm and bolsters courage.

Spellcasting

Iawynn can call upon Selûne for divine magic:

  • Protection Spells: Shield of faith, warding bond, protection from evil
  • Healing Magic: Cure wounds, lesser restoration
  • Detection: Detect evil and good, locate object
  • Radiant Power: Moonbeam, searing smite, branding smite
  • Support: Bless, aid, heroism

Divine Sense

Iawynn can sense the presence of powerful evil—fiends, undead, unholy objects. The ability resonates like moonlight revealing what hides in shadow.

Channel Divinity: Selûne's Gifts

  • Turn Undead: Command the walking dead to flee in Selûne's name
  • Moonfire: Channel radiant energy to harm enemies or light the darkest places
  • Lunar Guidance: Divine inspiration to aid allies or find the path forward

Relationship with Tyrael

Sisters of Light and Shadow

The bond between Iawynn and Tyrael is profound—forged by shared childhood, deepened by war, and tempered by their vastly different approaches to fighting evil.

Complementary Paths

Where Iawynn is light, Tyrael is shadow. Where Iawynn confronts evil openly with holy wrath, Tyrael eliminates it silently with psionic precision. Yet both serve the same cause: protecting the innocent and opposing darkness.

The Age Gap

Tyrael is approximately 80 years older than Iawynn—essentially, Tyrael helped raise her younger sister. This creates a dynamic where:

  • Tyrael is protective, sometimes overly so
  • Iawynn respects her sister's experience but chafes at being "watched over"
  • Tyrael sees the child she helped raise; Iawynn wants to be seen as an equal warrior
  • Both love each other deeply but occasionally clash over methods and autonomy

Different Methods, Shared Values

Iawynn's Perspective:

"Tyrael works in shadows because someone must. I do not always approve of her methods, but I trust her heart. She has saved more lives than she will ever claim credit for. While I stand in Selûne's light, she ensures no threat approaches from the darkness. We are two hands of the same purpose."

Potential Tensions:

  • Honor vs. Pragmatism: Iawynn values honorable combat; Tyrael uses assassination and deception
  • Transparency vs. Secrecy: Paladins are sworn to truth; Harpers thrive on secrets
  • Protection vs. Independence: Tyrael wants to shield Iawynn; Iawynn wants to prove herself
  • Public vs. Private: Iawynn is known and celebrated; Tyrael works best unnoticed

Unbreakable Bond:

Despite differences, the sisters would die for each other without hesitation. War proved that. They may argue over methods, but never over loyalty.

Roleplaying Notes

Personality Traits

  • Honorable: Strict adherence to paladin's oath and code of conduct
  • Courageous: Faces danger without hesitation, especially to protect others
  • Compassionate: Genuinely cares for the suffering and the lost
  • Determined: Once committed to a cause, will not yield
  • Proud: Not arrogant, but confident in her abilities and calling
  • Young (by elf standards): Still has some impulsiveness and idealism

Internal Conflicts

  • Sister's Protection: Grateful for Tyrael's love but wants to stand on her own
  • Methods vs. Results: Struggles when Tyrael's questionable methods achieve good outcomes
  • War's Weight: Carries guilt over those she couldn't save
  • Oath's Demands: Balancing mercy with the need to destroy evil
  • Public Figure: Being known and celebrated while Tyrael works unrecognized
  • Youthful Idealism: Learning that the world is more complex than light vs. dark

Speech & Mannerisms

  • Speaks with conviction and clarity—a voice used to inspiring troops
  • Often invokes Selûne's name and teachings
  • Addresses others with respect and courtesy
  • Direct and honest, sometimes blunt
  • Protective tone when discussing innocents or those in need
  • Slight exasperation when Tyrael is being overprotective
  • Reverent when discussing faith or holy matters

Combat Style

  • Frontline Fighter: Charges into battle, leading from the front
  • Defensive Protector: Positions herself between enemies and allies
  • Divine Striker: Uses smite abilities to destroy evil with holy power
  • Tactical Healer: Balances offense with healing wounded allies
  • Inspiring Presence: Her courage and faith bolster companions
  • Radiant Specialist: Especially effective against fiends, undead, and darkness

Character Development Paths

Potential Story Arcs

Arc 1: The Moonmaiden's Test

Selûne sends a divine vision or quest that tests Iawynn's faith, judgment, and compassion. Success could grant greater divine power or recognition within the Order of the Silver Ladies.

Arc 2: Shadow and Light

A situation forces Iawynn to rely on Tyrael's methods—deception, assassination, morally gray choices. Can she accept these methods? Can she forgive herself for using them?

Arc 3: Standing Alone

Separated from Tyrael, Iawynn must prove she can succeed independently. A mission where her sister cannot protect her from the shadows.

Arc 4: Cleansing the Darkness

Jotunn's Tears and other cursed sites become a personal crusade. Iawynn dedicates herself to purifying the region of infernal taint and ancient evil.

Arc 5: Return to Evermeet

Something calls the sisters back to their birthplace. Old secrets, family history, or a threat to the sacred isle itself requires their return.

Arc 6: The Cost of War

Trauma from the War of the Silver Marches resurfaces. Iawynn must confront survivor's guilt, loss, and the question of whether her sacrifices were worth it.

Final Notes

Iawynn represents the ideal of the paladin—courage, faith, and unwavering commitment to good. As a Silver Lady of Selûne, she stands as a beacon in dark places, a shield for the weak, and a sword against evil. Her youth (by elven standards) brings both idealism and room for growth, while her war experience has tempered her with hard-won wisdom. The relationship with her sister Tyrael provides rich roleplaying opportunities: mutual love and respect complicated by different methods and the challenge of stepping out from an elder sibling's protective shadow.

Where Tyrael works unseen, Iawynn shines openly. Together, they are unstoppable—one sister illuminates the path, the other guards it from the darkness that others never see.
