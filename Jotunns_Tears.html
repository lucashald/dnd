Jotunn's Tears

The Cursed Pool - Narfell's Darkest Legacy

Quick Reference

Type: Cursed Glacial Pool Location: High Glacial Cirque Elevation: ~10,000 feet Distance from Fjord's End: 2 days' climb Threat Level: Extreme Magic Type: Infernal Curse

⚠️ DM Warning ⚠️

This location is EXTREMELY dangerous and should be reserved for parties of level 7+.

The pool's curse can permanently transform or drive mad any creature that interacts with it. Players should be warned in-character through rumors, NPC warnings, and environmental clues that this is not a place to be taken lightly.

The Pool

The glacial pool stretches before you like a mirror of black ice, its surface so still it might be solid. But you can see the faint shimmer of movement beneath, and the water itself seems to pulse with a malevolent blue light that seems to come from deep below. Ancient runes are carved into the surrounding stone—giant script that predates any human settlement, though beneath them you can make out older, more sinister markings in a language that makes your eyes hurt to perceive. The air here is wrong. Too cold, too still, too quiet. No birds sing. No insects buzz. Even the wind seems reluctant to disturb this place. The water gives off a faint mist that clings to the ground like grasping fingers, and you could swear you hear distant whispers in languages older than memory.

Physical Description

  • Size: Roughly circular, approximately 200 feet in diameter
  • Depth: Unknown—appears bottomless, though the deepest anyone has measured is 300 feet
  • Water: Unnaturally still, midnight blue-black color, never freezes despite altitude
  • Temperature: The water itself is freezing (32°F), but radiates an unnatural chill that extends 50 feet
  • Light: Emits a faint bioluminescence in deep blue, visible at night, pulsing like a slow heartbeat
  • Sound: Absolute silence within 100 feet of the pool—no natural sounds occur here
  • Smell: Faint scent of sulfur and old copper (blood), stronger near the water's edge

The Shore

  • Smooth stone worn by ancient glacial action
  • Ring of dead earth 30 feet from the water—nothing grows here
  • Multiple sets of ancient carvings at different heights around the pool
  • Scattered bones of animals that died here (some mutated, all ancient)
  • Dark stains on the rocks that might be old blood or something worse

The Curse

Infernal Transformation

The pool's curse is ancient, cruel, and multifaceted. It was designed not just to punish, but to corrupt—to take the natural order and twist it into something grotesque and violent.

Primary Effect: Monstrous Mutation

Any creature that drinks from the pool undergoes a horrific transformation:

  • Size Increase: Grows to Huge size (or one size category larger if already Huge), approximately 10-20x original mass
  • Physical Changes: Body warps and changes—fur becomes matted with ice, eyes glow with unnatural light, claws and teeth enlarge
  • Elemental Affinity: Gains resistance or immunity to cold, radiates freezing aura
  • Strength Enhancement: Dramatic increase in physical power (Strength increases by 6-10 points)
  • Pain: Transformation is agonizing and never truly ends—creature is in constant pain
  • Duration: Permanent unless powerful magic is used to reverse it

Secondary Effect: Madness

The curse doesn't just change the body—it breaks the mind:

  • Rage: Overwhelming, uncontrollable fury at all living things
  • Hunger: Insatiable desire to consume, though nothing sates it
  • Paranoia: Believes everything is a threat or trying to steal from it
  • Confusion: Can't remember its former life, recognizes nothing familiar
  • Compulsion: Driven to return to the pool periodically, though drinking again worsens the curse
  • Susceptibility: Intelligent creatures suffer more—the curse feeds on complex thought

Progression

The curse worsens over time:

  • Hours 1-24: Transformation completes, pain is most intense, mind begins to fracture
  • Days 2-7: Madness takes hold, creature becomes violently aggressive
  • Weeks 2-4: Loses all memory of former existence, operates on pure instinct and rage
  • Months 2+: Additional mutations may occur, creature becomes walking disaster
  • Years 1+: Some victims become so changed they're barely recognizable as their original species

Game Mechanics

Drinking from the Pool:

  • Constitution Save DC 18 to resist initial effects
  • Failure: Transformation begins immediately, completes in 1d4 hours
  • Success: Creature takes 4d10 necrotic damage and is poisoned for 24 hours, but avoids transformation
  • Wisdom Save DC 16 (after transformation) to resist madness for 24 hours; must repeat daily
  • Repeated Exposure: Each additional drink lowers save DCs by 2 (cumulative)

Reversal

Breaking the curse is extremely difficult:

  • Greater Restoration cast at 7th level or higher (within first week only)
  • Wish or Divine Intervention (any time)
  • Special Ritual: Requires rare components, knowledge of the original pact, and breaking the devil's hold
  • Death and True Resurrection: Killing the creature and using True Resurrection bypasses the curse
  • The Devil's Release: Finding and defeating/negotiating with the devil who created the curse

History: The Narfell Noble's Bargain

The Empire of Narfell

Over a thousand years ago, the Empire of Narfell ruled these lands. The Narfelli were powerful demon-binders and infernalists, their empire built on pacts with dark powers from the Lower Planes. They raised great cities and commanded armies of bound demons, but their hubris would be their downfall.

Timeline

  • Circa -900 DR: The Narfell Empire at its height, controlling vast northern territories
  • Circa -700 DR: Lord Valthrun Kros builds his estate at what is now Jotunn's Tears
  • Circa -650 DR: Valthrun makes his pact with the devil Azarokh
  • Circa -160 DR: The Narfell Empire falls in war with Raumathar
  • Present Day: The pool remains cursed, a festering wound in reality

Lord Valthrun Kros and the Devil's Pact

The Noble

Lord Valthrun Kros was a Narfelli nobleman of moderate standing who craved greater power. His estate overlooked a pristine glacial tarn in the high mountains—a place of beauty and tranquility. But Valthrun was not content with beauty; he wanted dominion.

The Desire

  • Sought to become one of the great demon-binders of the empire
  • Wanted to command armies and conquer neighboring territories
  • Desired immortality and power beyond mortal ken
  • Envied rivals who had made successful infernal pacts
  • Believed he was clever enough to outwit a devil

The Devil: Azarokh the Corruptor

Azarokh was a pit fiend of considerable power who specialized in twisted bargains. He didn't just want souls—he wanted to create lasting monuments to corruption, places where evil would endure long after empires fell.

Azarokh's True Goal: Create a permanent source of cursed, maddened creatures that would plague the region for millennia, spreading chaos and fear long after Narfell's fall.

The Bargain

Valthrun summoned Azarokh and made his demand: power, longevity, and dominion over his enemies. The devil agreed, with terms:

"I grant you power beyond measure—strength to crush armies, years beyond counting, dominion over all who oppose you. In exchange, I ask but three small things: First, your estate and all upon it shall be mine when you tire of life. Second, the waters of your beautiful tarn shall carry my gift to all who drink. Third, when you finally embrace death, your essence shall join the waters, that you might witness my gift's work for all eternity."

The Deception

Valthrun, arrogant and foolish, agreed. He thought:

  • "I will never tire of life—I'll be immortal!"
  • "A gift to those who drink sounds beneficial—blessed waters for my people!"
  • "My essence in the water means I'll be remembered forever!"

He was wrong on all counts.

The Curse Manifests

  • Day One: Valthrun gains immense power—strength, vitality, magical might
  • Week One: His servants drink from the tarn, seeking to share his blessing—they transform into monsters
  • Week Two: The monsters, now mad, slaughter everyone in the estate including Valthrun's family
  • Month One: Valthrun, now alone and horrified, confronts the monsters—his newfound power lets him defeat them
  • Month Two: He realizes he cannot leave the estate—the pact binds him here
  • Year One: He begins to go mad from isolation and guilt
  • Year Ten: He can no longer bear existence and seeks death, but finds he cannot die
  • Year Fifty: Completely mad, he throws himself into the tarn, fulfilling the third clause

Valthrun's Fate

Valthrun's essence dissolved into the waters, becoming part of the curse. He remains there still, a fragment of consciousness trapped in eternal torment, forced to witness every creature that drinks and transforms. Some say his screams can be heard in the whispers that emanate from the pool. Others say his face appears in the water's depths, frozen in eternal anguish.

The Ruins of Valthrun's Estate

The estate that once stood here is long gone, but ruins remain:

  • Foundation Stones: Ancient blocks carved with Narfelli script and infernal runes
  • Collapsed Columns: Marble pillars now toppled and overgrown (with dead vegetation)
  • The Summoning Circle: Where Valthrun called Azarokh—still faintly active, dangerous to enter
  • Underground Chambers: Partially collapsed cellars and dungeons beneath the estate
  • The Pact Stone: A black basalt altar where the deal was sealed, inscribed with the terms in Infernal
  • Skeletal Remains: Bones of Valthrun's household, some showing signs of mutation

What Can Be Found

  • Ancient Narfelli artifacts and texts (mostly ruined)
  • The written pact between Valthrun and Azarokh (on the Pact Stone)
  • Clues to the curse's nature and possible reversal methods
  • Remnants of Valthrun's library—knowledge of demon-binding (cursed knowledge)
  • Personal effects revealing the tragedy that unfolded
  • Possibly: a way to contact or summon Azarokh (extremely dangerous)

The Giant Script

The False Legend

The pool is called "Jotunn's Tears" because of the giant-runes carved around its perimeter. These runes tell a story—but it's a lie, crafted by Azarokh to obscure the truth and add another layer of cruelty to his work.

The Carved Tale

The giant runes tell the story of a frost giant parent whose child drowned in the tarn. In grief, the giant wept into the waters for seven days and seven nights, cursing the pool so that any who drank from it would feel the pain of loss as the giant did.

This is completely false. The devil carved these runes to:

  • Obscure the true infernal nature of the curse
  • Create sympathy for a fictional tragedy
  • Misdirect anyone trying to understand or break the curse
  • Mock the concept of parental love by associating it with corruption

The Infernal Script

Beneath the giant runes, barely visible and painful to perceive, are the true markings—written in Infernal. These describe:

  • The actual terms of Valthrun's pact
  • The mechanism of the curse
  • Azarokh's true name (dangerous to speak)
  • Hints toward breaking the curse (deliberately cryptic)

Reading the Infernal Script:

  • Requires fluency in Infernal or Comprehend Languages
  • Wisdom save DC 14 or take 2d6 psychic damage from perceiving infernal truth
  • Reading Azarokh's true name: Wisdom save DC 16 or be Frightened for 1 minute
  • Complete translation requires several hours and risks attracting attention

Approaches to the Pool

Routes to Jotunn's Tears

The pool sits at approximately 10,000 feet elevation in a glacial cirque accessible by several routes, each with its own dangers.

Route 1: The Northern Climb (Standard)

  • Distance: 2 days from Fjord's End
  • Difficulty: Moderate to Difficult
  • Terrain: Steep slopes, scree fields, glacier crossing
  • Hazards: Avalanches, crevasses, altitude sickness, extreme cold
  • Stealth: Difficult—exposed approach visible from the pool
  • Recommended: For parties with mountain experience and proper equipment

Route 2: The Eastern Ridge (Difficult)

  • Distance: 1.5 days from Fjord's End
  • Difficulty: Very Difficult
  • Terrain: Rocky ridge, sheer cliffs, narrow ledges
  • Hazards: Falling (100+ foot drops), unstable rock, high winds
  • Stealth: Good—approach from above, less visible
  • Recommended: For parties with climbing skills or flight

Route 3: Through the Old Mine (Dangerous)

  • Distance: 1 day from Giantspire Peak mine entrance
  • Difficulty: Variable
  • Terrain: Ancient dwarven tunnels, collapsed passages, underground lakes
  • Hazards: Cave-ins, underground creatures, getting lost, toxic gases
  • Stealth: Excellent—emerge near the pool with cover
  • Recommended: For parties who have explored the mine and found the connection

Route 4: Magical Transport (Expensive)

  • Methods: Teleportation Circle, Wind Walk, Fly, etc.
  • Difficulty: Depends on resources
  • Hazards: Magical wards around the pool may interfere
  • Stealth: Variable—sudden arrival may trigger reactions
  • Recommended: For high-level parties or those with NPC assistance

Environmental Conditions

Altitude Effects

  • Thin Air: Constitution save DC 12 every hour or gain one level of exhaustion
  • Acclimatization: Spending 24 hours at 8,000+ feet grants advantage on saves
  • Spell Effects: Fire spells deal half damage due to reduced oxygen

Temperature

  • Base Temperature: 20-30°F during day, 0-10°F at night
  • Wind Chill: Can drop effective temperature to -20°F
  • Cold Exposure: Constitution save DC 10 every hour or gain exhaustion
  • Pool's Aura: Additional 10°F drop within 50 feet of water

Weather

  • Sudden Storms: Can develop in minutes at this elevation
  • Visibility: Fog, snow, or clouds can reduce visibility to 20 feet
  • Lightning: Exposed ridge is dangerous during storms

Tactical Considerations

The Battlefield

Terrain Features

  • The Pool: 200-foot diameter circle—falling in is extremely dangerous
  • Shore Ring: 30-foot band of smooth stone around the pool
  • Dead Zone: 30-foot ring of dead earth beyond the shore, difficult terrain
  • Boulder Field: Scattered glacial erratics provide cover (50-100 feet from pool)
  • Ruins: Foundation stones and collapsed structures offer partial cover and hiding spots
  • Cliff Walls: Cirque is surrounded by 100-300 foot cliffs on three sides
  • Glacier Tongue: Access point on north side, slippery and unstable

Advantages & Disadvantages

High Ground:

  • Cliff tops provide excellent ranged attack positions
  • Difficult for large creatures to reach
  • Risk of avalanche if fighting triggers rockfall

The Pool as Hazard:

  • Can be used to push enemies into cursed water
  • Transformed creatures may be drawn to defend it
  • Spells involving water may behave unpredictably
  • Swimming in it is suicide (instant exposure to curse)

Ruins as Cover:

  • Provides half or three-quarters cover
  • May contain trapped areas or cursed objects
  • The Summoning Circle is particularly dangerous

Environmental Hazards:

  • Extreme cold drains resources (spell slots for warmth)
  • Altitude affects stamina and concentration
  • Limited escape routes if things go wrong
  • Weather can change combat dynamics rapidly

Sight Lines

  • Pool is visible from approaching north route ~500 feet away
  • Eastern ridge approach gives view from above
  • Mine exit is concealed but opens near ruins (good surprise potential)
  • At night, pool's bioluminescence is visible from miles away

Inhabitants & Threats

What Lurks Here

Transformed Creatures

Various animals have drunk from the pool over the centuries. Some may still linger:

  • The Titan Bear: Most recent victim, now threatens Fjord's End
  • Cursed Goats: Mountain goats enlarged and maddened, incredibly aggressive
  • Monstrous Raven: Eagle-sized raven with freezing aura, drawn to shiny objects
  • The Ancient Elk: Huge, skeletal elk that's been cursed for decades, barely alive
  • Ice Wolves: Pack of wolves transformed together, hunt as corrupted unit

Valthrun's Echo

The spirit of Lord Valthrun sometimes manifests near the pool:

  • Appears as ghostly figure in Narfelli noble attire
  • Tries to warn people away (when lucid) or lure them in (when mad)
  • Can provide information about the curse and its history
  • May attack if he believes visitors are agents of Azarokh
  • Use Specter or Wraith statistics with custom abilities

Infernal Guardians

Azarokh left guardians to protect his work:

  • Ice Devils: 1-2 devils bound to the Summoning Circle
  • Invisible Stalker: Watches for intruders attempting to break the curse
  • Nightmare: Patrols the approaches, reports to Azarokh

Natural Hazards

  • Avalanche Risk: Loud noises (thunder, explosions) may trigger slides
  • Crevasses: Hidden under snow, especially near glacier
  • Altitude Sickness: Ongoing Constitution saves required
  • Hypothermia: Extended exposure without magical protection is deadly

Breaking the Curse

Methods to End Jotunn's Tears

Permanently ending the curse is a quest unto itself, requiring multiple steps:

Option 1: Fulfill the Pact

  • Find a way to contact Azarokh (via Summoning Circle or other means)
  • Negotiate release of the curse (requires something of equal value)
  • Possible trades: souls, service, spreading corruption elsewhere
  • WARNING: Dealing with pit fiends is extremely dangerous
  • Azarokh may demand the party help him escape imprisonment or perform dark deeds

Option 2: Divine Intervention

  • Seek aid from a powerful good deity
  • Requires completing a holy quest or pilgrimage
  • May need to transport large quantities of holy water to the pool
  • Ritual to consecrate the pool and drive out infernal presence
  • Could involve battle with devils sent to stop the purification

Option 3: Arcane Reversal

  • Study the Pact Stone and Infernal script to understand curse mechanics
  • Gather rare components: devil's blood, tears of a penitent fiend, etc.
  • Perform counter-ritual designed to unweave the curse
  • Requires high-level spellcaster (9th level spells or multiple 7th level casters)
  • Risky—failure might strengthen the curse or attract Azarokh's wrath

Option 4: Destroy the Pool

  • Physically obliterate the pool with massive force
  • Would require multiple Earthquakes, collapsing the cirque
  • Or: redirect glacier to bury it under ice permanently
  • WARNING: Cursed water might seep into groundwater, spreading curse
  • May only seal the problem rather than solve it

Option 5: Redeem Valthrun

  • Find and communicate with Valthrun's tortured spirit
  • Help him find peace, breaking his connection to the curse
  • May weaken the curse enough for other methods to work
  • Could involve recovering his remains and giving proper burial
  • Requires understanding his guilt and offering genuine forgiveness

Adventure Hooks

Quests Involving Jotunn's Tears

Low-Level (Preparation)

  • Research Mission: Kumari hires party to find texts about Narfell curses
  • Interview Survivors: Track down people who've seen the pool and lived
  • Gather Supplies: Acquire cold weather gear, holy water, etc.
  • Scout the Approaches: Map routes to the pool without going all the way

Mid-Level (Investigation)

  • The Titan's Thirst: Deal with creature that drank from the pool
  • First Expedition: Journey to the pool, study it from safe distance
  • Ruins Exploration: Search Valthrun's estate for clues
  • Valthrun's Story: Encounter his ghost, learn the history
  • Recover Artifacts: Retrieve Narfelli items needed for research

High-Level (Resolution)

  • Break the Curse: Attempt one of the permanent solutions
  • Confront Azarokh: Summon and deal with the devil
  • Holy Pilgrimage: Complete divine quest to purify the pool
  • The Final Ritual: Perform arcane ceremony to end the curse
  • Seal the Gateway: Close the connection between pool and Lower Planes

Ongoing Complications

  • Red Wizard Interest: Thayans want to study/weaponize the curse
  • Cultist Activity: Devil worshippers try to protect or exploit the pool
  • Cursed Creatures: More animals drink and transform, threatening region
  • Dreams and Visions: Pool reaches out psychically to potential victims
  • Azarokh's Plans: Devil becomes aware of attempts to break curse, interferes

DM Notes

Running Jotunn's Tears

Atmosphere

This location should feel oppressive, wrong, and dangerous:

  • Emphasize the unnatural silence—no birds, no wind, no natural sounds
  • Describe the pool's faint glow and the way it seems to pulse like breathing
  • Whispers in unknown languages at the edge of hearing
  • Sense of being watched by malevolent intelligence
  • Physical discomfort—cold that seeps into bones, headache from altitude
  • Time feels wrong here—hard to tell how long they've been present

Foreshadowing

Build dread before the party ever reaches the pool:

  • NPCs in Fjord's End tell dark legends about the place
  • Kumari has books describing Narfelli curses (without full details)
  • Encounter signs of transformed creatures on the approach
  • Find old bones and equipment of previous explorers
  • See the pool's glow from a distance at night

Pacing the Reveal

  • First Visit: Observe from distance, piece together giant-rune story (false legend)
  • Second Visit: Explore ruins, find Pact Stone, encounter Valthrun's ghost
  • Third Visit: Battle guardians, study Infernal script, begin planning cure
  • Final Visit: Execute plan to break curse, deal with Azarokh if necessary

Scaling Difficulty

  • Level 5-7: Focus on investigation, avoid direct conflict with curse
  • Level 8-10: Battle transformed creatures, explore ruins fully
  • Level 11-14: Confront infernal guardians, attempt to break curse
  • Level 15+: Deal with Azarokh directly, permanent resolution

Consequences

Actions at the pool have lasting effects:

  • Disturbing the Summoning Circle might alert Azarokh
  • Damaging the pool could release cursed water downstream
  • Learning Azarokh's true name gives power but draws attention
  • Successfully breaking curse makes party famous (and targets)
  • Failed attempts might strengthen the curse or create new problems
