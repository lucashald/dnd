Magnus Irongrip ♪

Dwarf Bard, Ship Captain, Harper Agent, Last of Earthfast

Character Overview

Race: Dwarf (Mountain Dwarf)
Age: 142 years
Class: Bard (College of Lore)
Occupation: Ship Captain, Harper Agent, Storyteller
Ship: The Silt Runner
Affiliation: The Harpers
Origin: Earthfast (destroyed 5 years ago)
Status: Active, Survivor

Physical Description

Magnus is a stout dwarf with the weathered appearance of one who has spent years at sea. His once-dark beard is now heavily streaked with grey, braided in the traditional style of Earthfast dwarves. Deep-set eyes the color of storm clouds carry both wisdom and sorrow, having witnessed the fall of his homeland.

  • Height: 4'6", broad-shouldered and sturdy
  • Build: Powerfully built, with the calloused hands of both a warrior and a sailor
  • Distinctive Features: Intricate braided beard with silver clan rings, a faded tattoo of Earthfast's seal on his right forearm, weathered sea-worn clothing
  • Typical Attire: Practical sailor's garb with a heavy sea cloak, leather boots reinforced for deck work, carries a well-worn lute across his back
  • Voice: Deep and resonant, with the accent of old Earthfast still strong despite years away
  • Mannerisms: Firm handshake that seems to assess character, tendency to hum old dwarven sailing songs, gazes at Giantspire Peak with a mix of pride and longing

The Fall of Earthfast

Five Years Ago: The Dragon's Wrath

Magnus's life changed forever when Putrifax, an ancient black dragon, descended upon Earthfast. The great dwarven city, carved deep into the mountains and standing for millennia, fell in a matter of days.

The Attack

  • Putrifax's Assault: The dragon emerged from the depths, bringing devastation with acid and fury
  • The City's Fall: Earthfast's legendary defenses, built to withstand sieges by armies, crumbled before draconic might
  • The Evacuation: Magnus was among those who helped organize the desperate flight of survivors
  • The Loss: Thousands perished; the great forges went cold; centuries of dwarven culture reduced to rubble
  • The Diaspora: Survivors scattered across the realm, carrying what they could of their heritage

Magnus's Escape

Magnus was leading a trade expedition when Putrifax attacked. Returning to find his home in ruins, he gathered what survivors he could find and used his ship to ferry them to safety. In those dark days, he acquired the vessel that would become the Silt Runner and took up the life of a wandering captain, unable to bear returning to what remained of Earthfast.

The Survivor's Burden

  • Carries the weight of being among the few who survived
  • Feels responsible for preserving Earthfast's memory and culture
  • Songs and stories of his homeland have taken on new importance—they are now acts of remembrance
  • Seeks news of other survivors and the dragon's current whereabouts
  • Dreams of one day seeing Earthfast reclaimed, though he knows it may be impossible

The Silt Runner

Magnus's Ship

The Silt Runner is a sturdy merchantman, built in the dwarven style with reinforced hull and deep keel. She's weathered countless storms and survived the dangerous waters of the northern fjords.

Ship Statistics

  • Type: Merchantman/Cargo vessel
  • Length: 80 feet
  • Crew: 8-12 (currently 10)
  • Cargo Capacity: 150 tons
  • Speed: Moderate, built for stability over speed
  • Construction: Dwarven-reinforced oak with iron fittings
  • Age: Acquired 5 years ago, originally built 30 years ago

Ship Features

  • Hidden Compartments: Concealed storage for Harper communications and sensitive cargo
  • Defensible: Reinforced hull, several ballistae, crew trained in defense
  • All-Weather: Built to handle the harsh northern seas
  • Captain's Cabin: Contains Magnus's personal effects, including relics from Earthfast
  • The Hearth: A small dwarven-style brazier in the hold where Magnus leads the crew in songs

Regular Routes

  • Fjord's End to various coastal settlements
  • Transports goods, news, and occasionally "special passengers" (Harper recruits or refugees)
  • Known for reliability and discretion
  • Accepts both legitimate trade and Harper-related missions

The Crew

Magnus's crew is loyal and diverse, including several other Earthfast refugees who followed him into exile. They function as much as a family as a working crew, bound together by shared loss and Magnus's leadership. Most know about his Harper connections, though they keep it quiet.

Heritage & Lineage

Irongrip Clan of Earthfast

Magnus comes from an ancient and distinguished line. The Irongrip clan has been part of Earthfast since its founding, renowned for three things: their skill as warriors, their mastery of song and story, and their ancestral connection to Giantspire Peak.

The Dragon-Slayer's Line

  • Ancient Glory: Magnus's ancestor wielded Snowpiercer and slew the white dragon of Giantspire Peak ~700 years ago
  • The Lost Mine: His family established the great mine in Giantspire before losing it to orcs 500 years ago
  • Family Legacy: Each generation has preserved the songs and stories of that heroic age
  • The Spear's Mystery: Magnus knows family legends about Snowpiercer but not its current location
  • Last of the Line: Magnus may be the last living Irongrip, as far as he knows

The Bardic Tradition

The Irongrip clan maintained an unusual tradition for dwarves—bardic practice. They believed that great deeds deserved great songs, and that history lived through story and music. Magnus is the latest in this line, trained from youth in the lore and songs of his people.

What Magnus Knows

  • Detailed oral histories of Giantspire Peak and the dragon slaying
  • Songs containing clues to the old mine's layout and defenses
  • Family legends about treasures and secrets left behind in the mountain
  • The location of some (but not all) mine entrances
  • Stories of what happened during the orc invasion
  • Ancient dwarven techniques for metalwork and stonecraft from Earthfast

Earthfast Relics

Magnus carries several items salvaged from Earthfast's ruins:

  • A clan banner, scorched but intact
  • His father's signet ring bearing the Irongrip seal
  • A book of songs and stories from the clan library
  • Tools from the family forge
  • A small stone carved with Earthfast's founding runes

Harper Activities

Agent of the Harpers ♪

Magnus joined the Harpers in the aftermath of Earthfast's fall. The organization offered him purpose when his home was gone—a way to fight against tyranny and protect others from suffering the same fate his people endured.

Recruitment

A Harper agent named Skylark found Magnus in the refugee camps, recognizing both his skills and his burning need for purpose. Magnus proved a natural fit—his bardic abilities, sailing expertise, and network of contacts made him invaluable.

Current Mission

  • Northern Watch: Monitor threats in the fjord regions and northern territories
  • Safe Passage: Transport Harper agents and refugees aboard the Silt Runner
  • Information Network: Gather intelligence from ports and coastal settlements
  • Fjord's End Development: Help establish the village as a Harper safe haven
  • Red Wizard Surveillance: Track Thayan activities, particularly around magical sites
  • Recruitment: Identify and vet potential Harper allies

Harper Network

  • Skylark: His recruiter and primary contact, based in Fjord's End
  • Coastal Agents: Network of informants in ports along his trade routes
  • Earthfast Survivors: Stays in contact with other refugees, some of whom are also Harpers
  • Regional Contacts: Knows Harper sympathizers throughout the north

Methods & Approach

  • Uses his cover as a merchant captain to move freely
  • Gathers information through songs and stories in taverns
  • Maintains plausible deniability—most see him as just a trader
  • Cautious about revealing Harper connections to outsiders
  • Assesses newcomers carefully before offering trust

Personality & Traits

Character Traits

  • Stoic Grief: Carries the weight of Earthfast's fall with quiet dignity
  • Protective: Fiercely defends those under his care, won't let another home fall if he can prevent it
  • Discerning: Judges character quickly and accurately through years of experience
  • Dutiful: Takes his responsibilities seriously, whether as captain, Harper, or last of his clan
  • Musical: Expresses deep emotions through song when words fail
  • Haunted: Dreams of Earthfast's fall, driven by survivor's guilt
  • Hopeful: Despite tragedy, still believes in fighting for a better world
  • Traditionalist: Honors dwarven customs and keeps Earthfast's memory alive

Quirks & Habits

  • Sings when seeing Giantspire Peak—a clan tradition and personal ritual
  • Offers a firm handshake and looks people directly in the eyes when meeting them
  • Maintains his beard braids in the old Earthfast style
  • Speaks of his lost city in present tense sometimes, as if it still stands
  • Carries his lute everywhere, even when it's impractical
  • Makes small offerings to Moradin at shrines along his route
  • Keeps a journal of survivor accounts and Earthfast memories

Motivations

  • Preservation: Keep Earthfast's culture and history alive through song and story
  • Protection: Prevent others from suffering as his people did
  • Vengeance: Deep desire to see Putrifax destroyed, though he knows he likely can't do it alone
  • Community: Build Fjord's End into a safe haven worthy of his ancestors
  • Legacy: Ensure the Irongrip name and deeds are not forgotten
  • Redemption: Atone for surviving when so many died

The Song of Giantspire

Magnus's Song

The sight of the mountain seems to stir something in the ship's captain. Magnus starts to sing, his deep voice carrying across the water:

A traditional Irongrip clan song, passed down for seven centuries:

"High above the frozen waters,
Where the mist-crowned giant sleeps,
Irongrip's son faced the dragon,
In the mountain's ancient deeps.

Snowpiercer true, forged in Earthfast,
Pierced the wyrm's cold heart of ice,
Mountain shook and glaciers trembled,
Paid in blood was freedom's price.

Stone and silver, deep we delved there,
Halls of craft beneath the peak,
Till the green-skins came with fire,
And we lost what we did seek.

But the mountain still remembers,
And the stones still bear our mark,
Irongrip's blood flows yet in me,
Guarding light against the dark."

The song serves multiple purposes: it's a history lesson, a family record, and a promise that the Irongrip legacy endures.

Relationships

Key Connections

In Fjord's End

  • Skylark: Harper contact and trusted friend. They share an understanding built on mutual respect and shared purpose.
  • Ingvar & Ingrid Runestone: Fellow dwarves who remind him of simpler times. He always stays at the Glacier's Rest when in port.
  • Kumari: Mutual respect with the lorekeeper. She's offered to help research Snowpiercer and Giantspire Peak multiple times. Magnus is both intrigued and wary—what she knows could be valuable or dangerous.
  • Tormund Stormwatch: Respects the scout's knowledge of the mountains and has hired him for expeditions.
  • Village Elders: Works with local leadership to improve Fjord's End's defenses and trade.

Beyond Fjord's End

  • Earthfast Survivors: Maintains contact with scattered refugees, sharing news and memories
  • Harper Network: Various agents throughout his trading routes
  • Merchant Contacts: Legitimate business relationships that also serve his Harper work
  • Dwarven Communities: Visits other dwarven settlements when possible, seeking connection to his heritage

Complex Feelings About

  • Putrifax: The dragon that destroyed his home. Magnus wants vengeance but knows he's likely not strong enough to face such a creature alone.
  • Giantspire Peak: His ancestral home and the site of his clan's greatest triumph. Being near it brings both pride and pain.
  • Potential Allies: Assesses newcomers as possible Harper recruits or threats to his mission.

Connection to Putrifax

The Dragon That Destroyed Earthfast

Putrifax is an ancient black dragon of terrifying power. Five years ago, the dragon emerged from the depths beneath Earthfast and laid waste to the city. Magnus's life has been defined by this event ever since.

What Magnus Knows

  • The Attack: Witnessed the aftermath, heard survivor accounts of the dragon's assault
  • Putrifax's Nature: Ancient, cunning, and cruel even by dragon standards
  • Current Location: Rumored to be in the Stone Forest region, possibly bound by Red Wizards
  • The Binding: Has heard reports that Thayan wizards have enslaved the dragon
  • Irony of Fate: The dragon that destroyed his home may now be a weapon for an even greater evil

Magnus's Dilemma

Magnus struggles with conflicting desires regarding Putrifax:

  • Wants to see the dragon destroyed for what it did to Earthfast
  • Recognizes he's not powerful enough to face such a creature
  • Wonders if the dragon's enslavement by the Red Wizards is justice or a greater threat
  • Fears what might happen if Putrifax breaks free of Thayan control
  • Seeks powerful allies who might help him achieve vengeance

Plot Hook

If the PCs prove trustworthy and capable, Magnus might reveal his connection to Putrifax and his desire to see the dragon destroyed. This could lead to missions investigating the dragon's current state, gathering intelligence on the Red Wizards, or even an eventual confrontation with the beast that destroyed Earthfast.

Harper Recruitment

  • Skylark's Friends: His quote about "Any friend of Skylark's" opens dialogue about Harper activities
  • Passage and Purpose: Offers free or discounted transport in exchange for Harper-aligned missions
  • Recruitment Quest: Asks PCs to help identify or protect a Harper asset

Earthfast Legacy

  • Survivor Search: Magnus learns of other Earthfast refugees in danger; needs help rescuing them
  • Relic Recovery: Hires PCs to retrieve specific items from Earthfast's ruins
  • Cultural Preservation: Asks PCs to help him document Earthfast's history and traditions
  • The Memorial: Wants to create a monument to fallen Earthfast; needs materials or protection

Giantspire Peak

  • Ancestral Pilgrimage: Magnus wants to explore his family's old mine but needs capable escorts
  • Snowpiercer's Search: Reveals family legends about the spear's location; seeks help finding it
  • The Dragon's Hoard: Believes his ancestor's treasure may still be hidden in Giantspire
  • Reclaim the Halls: Dreams of retaking the Irongrip mine as a new home for refugees

Putrifax Connection

  • Intelligence Gathering: Needs information about Putrifax's current location and status
  • Red Wizard Investigation: Wants to understand how Thayans bound the dragon
  • Earthfast Expedition: Hires PCs to explore the ruins, perhaps to understand how the dragon emerged
  • Vengeance Quest: If PCs prove powerful enough, might reveal his ultimate goal of destroying Putrifax

The Silt Runner

  • Crew Troubles: Needs help dealing with a problem among his sailors
  • Cargo Protection: Hires PCs as guards for a dangerous voyage
  • Ship Repairs: Silt Runner damaged; needs rare materials or skilled labor
  • Mutiny: A crew member has been compromised by enemies; Magnus needs help resolving it

Roleplaying Magnus

Voice & Mannerisms

Speech Patterns

  • Deep, resonant voice with the accent of old Earthfast
  • Speaks deliberately, choosing words with care
  • Uses dwarven proverbs and sayings from his homeland
  • Occasionally slips into Dwarvish when emotional
  • References songs and stories in conversation

Body Language

  • Stands with a sailor's balance, comfortable with the ship's motion
  • Direct eye contact when assessing someone
  • Firm handshake that seems to measure character
  • Touches his lute when thinking or distressed
  • Gazes at Giantspire Peak with visible emotion

Sample Dialogue

Meeting PCs for the first time:
"Welcome aboard the Silt Runner. I'm Magnus Irongrip, her captain." *extends hand for a firm handshake* "Any friend of Skylark's has friends here. Come, we've the wind and the tide—let's put this port behind us."
When asked about Earthfast:
*long pause, hand straying to his beard braids* "Earthfast... was home. Greatest of dwarven cities, standing for three thousand years. Until five years ago, when Putrifax came from below. I... wasn't there when she fell. Part of me wishes I had been. The other part knows we needed survivors to remember." *voice hardens* "And to ensure the dragon pays for what it did."
When seeing Giantspire Peak:
*begins to sing without preamble, voice carrying across the water* "High above the frozen waters, where the mist-crowned giant sleeps..." *after finishing* "My ancestor stood on that peak seven centuries ago and slew a dragon with Snowpiercer. The mountain remembers, even if the world forgets."
Assessing PCs for Harper work:
"I carry more than trade goods on the Silt Runner. Sometimes I carry people who need safe passage, or messages that can't be entrusted to common couriers. Dangerous work, but necessary. You seem capable—and Skylark seems to trust you. Interested in earning more than just passage?"
On his burden as last of his line:
"I may be the last Irongrip. Last one who knows the songs, the stories, the way we braided our beards or forged our steel. Heavy burden, aye? But someone must carry it. Someone must remember. If not me, then who?"

Musical Abilities

Magnus the Bard

Bardic Training

Magnus was trained in the Irongrip family tradition, combining dwarven lore-keeping with bardic magic. His style is distinctly dwarven—deep-voiced, rhythmic, and focused on preserving history through song.

Instruments

  • Lute: His primary instrument, carved from Earthfast oak and inlaid with silver
  • Drums: Knows traditional dwarven percussion for work songs and battle chants
  • Voice: His deep, resonant singing voice is his greatest instrument

Repertoire

  • Earthfast Songs: Historical ballads and cultural songs from his homeland
  • Irongrip Clan Songs: Family histories, including the Song of Giantspire
  • Sea Shanties: Working songs learned during his years as captain
  • Harper Songs: Code songs and rallying anthems of the organization
  • Laments: Mourning songs for fallen cities and lost friends

Bardic Magic

As a bard of moderate power, Magnus can:

  • Inspire courage and bolster allies through song
  • Use cutting words to demoralize and distract foes
  • Cast spells related to knowledge, communication, and charm
  • Use his music to heal minor wounds and cure ailments
  • Detect magic and identify magical items through old dwarven songs

Combat & Capabilities

In Battle

Fighting Style

While primarily a bard, Magnus is also a capable warrior in the dwarven tradition. He fights with:

  • Battleaxe: A well-maintained weapon from his family's forge
  • Handaxe: For throwing or close work
  • Crossbow: Practical for shipboard combat
  • Bardic Magic: Supports allies and hinders enemies with spells

Tactical Approach

  • Supports and inspires allies rather than seeking glory
  • Uses terrain and positioning learned from naval combat
  • Fights defensively, protecting those under his care
  • Saves his most powerful magic for critical moments
  • Will retreat if it saves lives, but never abandons his people

Limitations

  • Not built for frontline combat despite dwarven resilience
  • Magic is moderate-level; he's not a powerhouse spellcaster
  • Age and experience make him cautious, not reckless
  • Protective nature can be exploited by threatening others

DM Notes

Using Magnus in Your Campaign

Role in the Story

  • Quest Giver: Provides Harper missions and transport to adventure locations
  • Mentor Figure: Can train PCs in bardic arts or dwarven lore
  • Connection to Larger Plots: Links to Putrifax, Red Wizards, and Giantspire Peak
  • Emotional Anchor: His tragedy and determination can inspire PCs
  • Resource Provider: Offers transport, information, and Harper network access

Character Development

Magnus's arc can evolve based on PC actions:

  • May find hope for reclaiming Giantspire Peak as a new home
  • Could discover Snowpiercer and face the choice of whether to use it
  • Might find other Irongrip survivors
  • May get opportunity to confront Putrifax
  • Could become a major Harper leader in the region

Scaling for Party Level

  • Low Level (1-4): Provides transport and simple information
  • Mid Level (5-10): Offers Harper missions, reveals Giantspire connections
  • High Level (11+): Involves PCs in Putrifax plot, major Harper operations

Secrets to Reveal Over Time

  • Full extent of his Harper activities
  • The Irongrip clan's connection to Snowpiercer
  • His personal desire for vengeance against Putrifax
  • Hidden knowledge about Giantspire Peak's secrets
  • Other Earthfast survivors and their current situations
