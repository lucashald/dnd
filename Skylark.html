Skylark ♪

Harper Druid and Protector of the Northern Reaches (Appears as a Ranger)

Character Overview

Race: Human
Class: Druid (Appears to be a Ranger)
Affiliation: The Harpers
Role: Agent, Scout, Information Gatherer
Status: Active
Location: Fjord's End and surrounding territories

Background

Skylark is a seasoned Harper agent operating in the northern reaches of the region. Known for his keen observational skills and deep knowledge of the wilderness, he has established Fjord's End as one of his primary bases of operation. His network extends throughout the region, with contacts in various settlements and communities.

Skylark's true nature as a druid is carefully concealed. To most observers, including many townspeople, he appears to be a skilled ranger—a wanderer who braves the harsh northern elements with practical gear and keen wilderness knowledge. In truth, Skylark's command of natural magic and his deep spiritual connection to the land provide far more protection than mundane clothing. His apparent lack of heavy cold-weather gear often goes unquestioned due to his mastery of druidic magic and the magical artifacts he wears.

As a druid, Skylark combines his communion with nature, his command of natural magic, and his deep Harper ideals of gathering intelligence, opposing tyranny, and protecting the innocent. He has been instrumental in monitoring the activities of the Red Wizards of Thay and other threats to the region's stability. His druidic nature gives him unique insights into unnatural disturbances and supernatural dangers threatening the balance of the land.

Appearance & Deception

Visual Impression: Skylark appears to be a typical northern ranger—practical clothing, well-worn gear, and the bearing of someone who spends more time in the wilderness than civilization. Most would not give him a second glance beyond noting his competence and self-sufficiency.

Notable Detail - The Ring: The most distinctive thing about Skylark, to those who look closely, is a subtle ring he wears on his left hand. This is his Ring of Warmth, a magical artifact that maintains a comfortable temperature around him. Those who know what to look for recognize it as a sign of magical expertise—though they might assume it belongs to a ranger who found it or acquired it through adventure.

Missing Cold Weather Gear: Observant individuals may notice that despite the harsh northern climate, Skylark rarely wears heavy furs, multiple layers, or the usual cold-weather equipment other travelers rely upon. When asked about this apparent oversight, he attributes it to his toughness, good health, or simply being well-acclimated to the north. In truth, the Ring of Warmth (combined with his druidic resilience) makes such mundane protections unnecessary.

The Deception: Skylark deliberately maintains the "ranger" appearance to avoid drawing attention to his true nature as a druid. Druids in the region are often associated with the Circle of Leth and its opposition to settlements and commerce. By appearing as a ranger rather than a druid, Skylark can move through trading towns and settlements without triggering suspicion or conflict. Only those he explicitly reveals his true nature to understand that his power comes from communion with nature itself, not ranger skills.

Personality & Traits

  • Observant: Little escapes Skylark's notice, whether in the wilderness or in social situations
  • Cautious: Years of covert operations have made him careful about revealing information
  • Loyal: Fiercely devoted to the Harper cause and those he considers allies
  • Resourceful: Makes use of every tool at his disposal, including his animal companion
  • Patient: Willing to observe and wait for the right moment to act
  • Deceptive (in service to good): Maintains a false identity for operational security; willing to deceive to protect the Harper cause

Connections

Harper Network

  • Magnus Irongrip: Fellow Harper agent and trusted ally. Magnus serves as Skylark's contact when bringing new recruits or allies to Fjord's End
  • Regional Harpers: Part of a larger network monitoring threats in the northern territories
  • Local Contacts: Has established relationships with key figures in Fjord's End and nearby settlements

Current Activities

Skylark is currently focused on:

  • Monitoring Red Wizard activities near the Stone Forest
  • Investigating ancient magical sites like the Cursed Pool of Jotunn's Tears
  • Establishing Fjord's End as a Harper safe haven
  • Gathering intelligence on regional threats and supernatural phenomena
  • Identifying potential allies and recruits for the Harper cause

Animal Companion: Adam

Adam the Raven

Species: Raven (Corvus corax)

Role: Scout, messenger, and Skylark's eyes and ears

Characteristics

  • Intelligence: Exceptionally clever, even for a raven
  • Loyalty: Devoted to Skylark, their bond forged over many years
  • Observant: Often perches at strategic locations to watch arrivals and departures
  • Communication: Can carry simple messages and relay basic information through behavior
  • Distinctive Features: Glossy black plumage with a knowing, intelligent gaze

Typical Behavior

Adam is frequently seen perching on the pier at Fjord's End, watching ships arrive. To the casual observer, he appears to be just another raven, but those who know Skylark recognize Adam as an extension of the ranger's vigilance. The raven's presence often signals that Skylark is aware of new arrivals, even when the ranger himself is not immediately visible.

"A raven perches on the pier, watching your arrival."

Harper Operations

Mission Priorities

  1. Intelligence Gathering: Monitor threats including Red Wizards, supernatural dangers, and political instability
  2. Network Building: Establish relationships with trustworthy individuals throughout the region
  3. Safe Haven Creation: Develop Fjord's End as a secure location for Harper operations
  4. Threat Assessment: Investigate ancient magical sites and their potential dangers
  5. Community Protection: Work to keep remote settlements safe from various threats

Roleplaying Skylark

Mannerisms

  • Often glances at Adam before speaking, as if consulting with the raven
  • Speaks in measured tones, choosing words carefully
  • Has a habit of observing people's reactions before revealing more information
  • Uses nature metaphors and references in conversation
  • Maintains awareness of his surroundings even during casual conversation

Knowledge Base

  • Wilderness Lore: Expert knowledge of the region's geography, flora, and fauna
  • Ancient History: Familiar with giant lore and old magical sites
  • Current Events: Well-informed about regional politics and threats
  • Harper Intelligence: Access to information from the wider Harper network

Potential Plot Hooks

  • May approach PCs as potential Harper recruits or allies
  • Can provide information about regional threats in exchange for assistance
  • Might request help investigating locations too dangerous to scout alone
  • Could serve as a quest-giver for Harper-aligned missions
  • May need aid protecting Fjord's End or other settlements

Relationship with Fjord's End

Skylark has cultivated Fjord's End as more than just a base of operations—he genuinely cares about the settlement and its inhabitants. The village's isolated location makes it ideal for covert Harper activities, while its natural defenses and loyal population make it a potential safe haven for those fleeing persecution or tyranny.

The locals know Skylark as a helpful ranger who warns them of dangers and occasionally brings trade or travelers their way. Most don't know about his Harper affiliation, seeing him simply as a protector of the region. Only Magnus Irongrip and perhaps a few other trusted individuals know his true role.

DM Notes

Using Skylark in Your Campaign

  • Mystery Introduction: PCs may notice Adam watching them before they ever meet Skylark
  • Gradual Reveal: Skylark's Harper identity doesn't need to be immediately revealed
  • Information Broker: Can provide key intelligence about regional threats when appropriate
  • Recruitment Opportunity: May offer PCs a chance to work with the Harpers
  • Trust Building: Magnus's introduction ("Any friend of Skylark's") suggests the PCs have already been vetted
  • Absent Presence: Skylark can be "away on Harper business" when convenient, with Adam serving as his representative
