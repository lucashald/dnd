Tharchion Nyth

Red Wizard of Enchantment & Dragon Master

Controller of Putrifax • Northern Tharchion • Master Manipulator

Tharchion Nyth moves through the world like a chess master arranging pieces on a board. Of average height but commanding presence, she wears her crimson robes like armor and her authority like a crown. Her shaved head bears tattoos of mesmerizing patterns that seem to shift when you're not looking directly at them. When she speaks, people listen—not because she raises her voice, but because her words carry the weight of absolute confidence backed by absolute power. She has controlled Putrifax for three consecutive years, a feat no other Red Wizard has accomplished, and she shows no signs of relinquishing that control.

Character Overview

Race: Human (Mulan)
Age: 52 years
Class: Wizard (School of Enchantment)
Level: 14
Title: Tharchion (Regional Governor) & Red Wizard
Specialization: Enchantment (Mind Control & Charm)
Position: Current Controller of Putrifax (3rd year)
Territory: Northern Thayan Operations (Sword Coast region)
Status: Active, Ascendant, Dangerous

Physical Description

  • Build: Average height (5'7") with a commanding, upright posture that makes her seem taller
  • Head: Shaved and tattooed with hypnotic patterns in deep purple and silver ink—spirals, eyes, and geometric designs that seem to move
  • Eyes: Pale grey, almost silver, with an unsettling quality of seeming to see through deception
  • Skin: Bronze-brown Mulan complexion, unblemished and well-maintained
  • Distinguishing Features: A silver ring through her left eyebrow, perfectly manicured hands, never appears disheveled even after days of ritual work
  • Attire: Crimson robes of exquisite quality, embroidered with silver and gold thread in patterns of eyes and chains. Wears a ceremonial dragon-scale cloak when exercising authority over Putrifax
  • Voice: Smooth and measured, with subtle undertones that make people want to agree with her
  • Presence: Commands attention effortlessly; people often find themselves deferring to her without realizing why

Background & History

Rise to Power

Nyth's ascent through Thayan hierarchy is a masterclass in political maneuvering combined with genuine magical talent. Unlike many Red Wizards who rely primarily on raw power, Nyth has always understood that the right word at the right time to the right person is worth more than a dozen fireballs.

Early Life & Training

  • Age 8: Identified as having magical talent; showed particular affinity for influencing others even as a child
  • Age 14: Entered Red Wizard academy, specializing in Enchantment despite its reputation as a "lesser" school
  • Age 21: Earned Red Wizard title by successfully charming a beholder long enough to negotiate a pact (the beholder was later killed by others, of course)
  • Age 25: Gained notice by mediating a dispute between two Zulkirs, demonstrating political acumen

Path to Tharchion

  • Age 30: Appointed as assistant to the previous Northern Tharchion
  • Age 35: The previous Tharchion died in a "tragic ritual accident" (Nyth's involvement was suspected but never proven)
  • Age 36: Appointed Tharchion of Northern Operations by Szass Tam himself, recognizing her competence
  • Age 44: First participated in the dragon binding ritual; carefully observed for a full year before attempting to control Putrifax
  • Age 47-49: Successfully gained control of Putrifax for three consecutive years—an unprecedented achievement

Achievements

  • Only Red Wizard to control Putrifax three years in a row
  • Pacified three barbarian tribes through a combination of dragon threats and mental compulsion
  • Increased Thayan influence along the Sword Coast by 40% during her tenure
  • Personally recruited or compromised seventeen key figures in northern politics
  • Maintains a network of charmed and dominated agents throughout the region

School of Magic: Enchantment

Mistress of Mind and Will

Nyth is a virtuoso of Enchantment magic, understanding that true power lies not in destroying enemies but in making them serve you. Her specialty is not brute-force domination but subtle manipulation that makes victims believe they're acting of their own will.

Enchantment Mastery Features

  • Instinctive Charm: As a reaction, can attempt to charm a creature attacking her
  • Split Enchantment: When casting an enchantment spell targeting one creature, can target a second creature
  • Alter Memories: Can make charmed creatures forget events that occurred while charmed
  • Extended Duration: Her enchantment spells last longer than normal

Signature Spells

Control & Domination:

  • Geas - Her tool for controlling Putrifax; she has mastered its application
  • Dominate Monster - For when subtlety isn't required
  • Mass Suggestion - Crowd control and diplomatic manipulation
  • Modify Memory - Covers her tracks expertly
  • Power Word: Stun - Her favorite combat opener

Subtle Manipulation:

  • Charm Person/Monster - Cast reflexively and often
  • Suggestion - Delivered so smoothly victims rarely realize they've been influenced
  • Crown of Madness - For sowing chaos among enemies
  • Compulsion - Battlefield control

Protection:

  • Mind Blank - She maintains this on herself during important operations
  • Globe of Invulnerability - Protection from lesser magic
  • Counterspell - Used strategically

Unique Techniques

Layered Control: Nyth often places multiple enchantments on important targets—a charm backed by a suggestion backed by a geas. This redundancy makes breaking her control extremely difficult.

Social Engineering: Combines magic with mundane manipulation. Often uses minor charms to make people more receptive, then convinces them through natural conversation, so they never realize they were enchanted.

Control of Putrifax

The Dragon Master

Nyth's control of Putrifax is her greatest achievement and the foundation of her current power. She succeeded where others failed by understanding a crucial truth: you cannot simply overpower an ancient dragon's will. You must outthink it.

The Strategy

"Others exhaust themselves trying to break through the dragon's defenses. Fools. You don't break a mountain—you wait for it to crack, then you slide a blade into the fissure. I let my colleagues tire themselves and Putrifax alike. I watch. I wait. And when the moment is perfect, when the dragon's mental shields are worn thin from dozens of failed attacks, I strike with precision rather than power. Three years running, and the beast is mine."

- Tharchion Nyth (private communication)

How She Succeeds

  • Patience: Waits while other wizards exhaust themselves and weaken Putrifax's resistance
  • Timing: Strikes at the precise moment when the dragon's defenses are thinnest
  • Preparation: Spends the entire year between rituals studying Putrifax's psychology and magical defenses
  • Layered Approach: Her geas is reinforced with subtle suggestion spells that make Putrifax more receptive
  • Political Savvy: Some suspect she makes deals with other participants (Thessarian's suspicions aren't entirely wrong)

The Binding Ceremony

The seven-day ritual requires all participants to be present, but only one wizard's geas ultimately takes hold. Nyth has refined her approach over three years:

  • Days 1-3: She makes token attempts, seemingly failing like the others, while actually studying Putrifax's resistance patterns
  • Days 4-5: She "rests" and lets others continue their futile attempts
  • Day 6: When Putrifax's mental defenses are worn thin and other wizards are exhausted, she makes her carefully-timed strike
  • Day 7: She reinforces the binding while others recover

Use of the Dragon

With Putrifax under her control, Nyth has:

  • Destroyed three settlements that resisted Thayan influence
  • Intimidated barbarian tribes into submission
  • Used the dragon as a strategic asset in five major operations
  • Deployed Putrifax to destroy Earthfast five years ago (on orders from higher authority)
  • Kept the dragon on a "short leash"—never allowing it far from where she can maintain the binding

The Dragon's Hatred

Putrifax loathes Nyth with intensity beyond what an immortal being typically feels. The dragon remembers two thousand years of imprisonment, and now thirty-six years of slavery. Nyth knows that if the binding ever breaks, Putrifax will come for her first. This knowledge doesn't frighten her—it amuses her. She views it as proof of her power.

Personality & Traits

Character Traits

  • Supremely Confident: Believes in her own superiority backed by genuine competence
  • Patient: Willing to wait years for the perfect moment to act
  • Manipulative: Sees every interaction as an opportunity to gain advantage
  • Strategic: Thinks in terms of long-term political and magical positioning
  • Ruthless: Will eliminate threats without hesitation but prefers to turn enemies into unwitting allies
  • Charismatic: Naturally compelling even without magic
  • Observant: Notices details others miss, especially about people's motivations and weaknesses
  • Ambitious: Eyes the position of Zulkir, though she's careful not to show it openly

Quirks & Mannerisms

  • Smiles slightly when she knows something others don't
  • Has a habit of tilting her head when studying someone, like a predator sizing up prey
  • Speaks in carefully constructed sentences, every word chosen for effect
  • Never appears rushed or flustered, maintaining perfect composure
  • Collects intelligence like others collect treasure—constantly gathering information
  • Maintains a pristine appearance even in adverse conditions

Motivations

  • Primary Goal: Maintain control of Putrifax indefinitely
  • Secondary Goal: Expand her territory and influence
  • Long-term Ambition: Become Zulkir of Enchantment (currently planning how to accomplish this)
  • Personal Drive: Prove Enchantment is the most powerful school of magic through demonstrable results
  • Political Aim: Position herself as indispensable to Szass Tam

Relationships & Politics

Key Connections

Szass Tam (Superior)

The Zulkir of Necromancy and ruler of Thay. Nyth has his attention and favor—something she has cultivated carefully. She provides regular reports on northern operations and ensures her successes are visible while her failures are quietly corrected. She suspects Szass Tam appreciates her competence and lack of obvious treachery (unlike some Red Wizards).

Thessarian Vekt (Rival)

The Conjurer's resentment is obvious and somewhat amusing to Nyth. She doesn't actually bribe the other ritual participants—she doesn't need to. Her magical skill and timing are genuine. However, she's careful to maintain the appearance that such bribes are possible, as it keeps rivals focused on the wrong thing. She views Thessarian as competent but limited by his rigid academic approach and emotional responses.

Kasec the Transmuter (Colleague)

A fellow ritual participant. Nyth has cultivated a cordial working relationship with him. She respects his power but considers him reckless. Has subtly encouraged his more impulsive tendencies.

Other Ritual Participants

The five other Red Wizards who participate in the binding. Nyth has carefully built relationships with each:

  • Two she has genuine alliances with based on mutual benefit
  • One she has enchanted to be more favorably disposed toward her (subtle, long-term charm)
  • Two remain neutral but respect her success

Current Zulkir of Enchantment

Zulkir Lallara Mediocros holds the position Nyth wants. Their relationship is formally respectful but privately tense. Lallara knows Nyth is a threat; Nyth knows Lallara knows. Both are too skilled at political maneuvering to make obvious moves against each other.

Putrifax

The dragon is her greatest weapon and her greatest danger. Their relationship is purely one of magical domination—there is no trust, no respect, only the geas that binds the dragon to obey. Nyth is constantly aware that if her control ever slips, even for a moment, Putrifax will kill her.

Current Activities & Projects

Active Operations

Northern Territory Management

  • Oversees all Thayan operations along the Sword Coast
  • Maintains network of charmed agents in key settlements
  • Coordinates with other Tharchions on larger Thayan strategic goals
  • Manages the Stone Forest research outpost and other facilities
  • Gathers intelligence on Harper activities (considers them a minor nuisance currently)

Dragon Deployment

  • Uses Putrifax as strategic deterrent and occasional weapon
  • Careful not to overuse the dragon—maintaining the binding requires focus
  • Recently authorized the Earthfast attack (five years ago) as demonstration of power
  • Considers where best to deploy the dragon for maximum political benefit

Preparation for Fourth Year

The next binding ritual is approaching. Nyth is preparing to control Putrifax for an unprecedented fourth consecutive year:

  • Studying the dragon's patterns and resistance evolution
  • Reinforcing her political alliances among the ritual participants
  • Researching methods to make the binding even stronger
  • Quietly gathering information about what competitors are planning

Long-term Strategy

  • Path to Zulkir: Building the case that her success with Putrifax proves Enchantment's supremacy
  • Expansion: Slowly increasing Thayan influence without triggering major opposition
  • Information Network: Establishing charmed agents in positions of influence throughout the region
  • Contingencies: Always has backup plans; currently developing a fail-safe if she ever loses control of Putrifax

Combat Abilities

In Battle

Combat Philosophy

Nyth avoids direct combat when possible, preferring to have others fight for her. When forced into battle, she relies on control, not damage.

Tactics

  • Pre-Battle: Casts Mind Blank on herself; has contingency spells prepared
  • Opening: Power Word: Stun on the most dangerous opponent, or Mass Suggestion to turn enemies against each other
  • Mid-Battle: Dominates key enemies, uses enchanted servants as shields
  • Protection: Globe of Invulnerability, Counterspell, Instinctive Charm (reaction)
  • Escape: Has pre-planned teleportation circles and Dimension Door routes
  • Ultimate Option: If truly threatened, summons Putrifax (though this risks weakening her binding)

Strengths

  • Can turn enemies into allies mid-combat
  • High-level spellcaster with deep spell repertoire
  • Always has contingency plans and escape routes
  • Can call upon Putrifax if desperate (though she'd prefer not to)
  • Excellent at recognizing when to retreat

Weaknesses

  • Physically frail—relies entirely on magic and minions
  • Mind Blank protects her but also limits some of her own divination abilities
  • Maintaining control of Putrifax requires concentration and mental reserves
  • Arrogance might make her underestimate unconventional threats
  • If separated from resources and minions, less dangerous in immediate combat (though still formidable)

Plot Hooks & Adventure Ideas

Ways to Involve Tharchion Nyth

The Dragon's Shadow

PCs discover a settlement destroyed by Putrifax (similar to Earthfast). Investigating leads them to discover Nyth controls the dragon. Must decide whether to try to disrupt her control, destroy the dragon, or find another solution.

Charmed Agent

An NPC the PCs trust has been enchanted by Nyth and is unknowingly feeding her information or setting up betrayals. PCs must discover the enchantment and find a way to break it without alerting Nyth.

Political Intrigue

Harper agents (Magnus, Skylark) ask PCs to infiltrate one of Nyth's diplomatic meetings. She's attempting to charm or dominate a regional leader. PCs must prevent this without starting an open war with Thay.

The Rival's Gambit

Thessarian secretly contacts the PCs with a proposal: help him gain control of Putrifax during the next binding ritual, and he'll use the dragon to accomplish something beneficial. Of course, Nyth learns of this and begins counter-moves. PCs caught in the middle.

Break the Binding

High-level campaign arc: PCs seek to free Putrifax from Thayan control. This puts them directly against Nyth, one of the most powerful Red Wizards in the region. Success would eliminate Thay's greatest weapon but might release an enraged ancient dragon.

The Enchantress's Offer

Nyth, having heard of the PCs' capabilities, makes them an offer: work for her on a specific mission, and she'll provide valuable intelligence or assistance. Can they trust a Red Wizard? Can they afford to refuse? What are the long-term costs?

Disrupting the Ritual

Epic-level: PCs must infiltrate and sabotage the annual dragon binding ritual. Success could free Putrifax, allow a different (possibly worse) Red Wizard to control it, or create chaos in Thayan ranks. Nyth will be present and extremely dangerous.

Roleplaying Nyth

Voice & Mannerisms

Speech Patterns

  • Speaks smoothly and confidently, with subtle persuasive undertones
  • Often phrases commands as questions or suggestions
  • Uses people's names frequently (a manipulation technique)
  • Never raises her voice—quiet confidence is more intimidating
  • Tends to answer questions with questions to maintain control of conversations

Sample Dialogue

Meeting PCs for the first time:
*slight smile, head tilted* "How interesting. Adventurers in my territory, and yet no one sent word of your arrival. Tell me—and do be honest, it's so much easier that way—what brings you to this particular corner of the world? I find I'm quite curious about you."
When threatened:
*genuinely amused* "Threaten me? Truly?" *light laugh* "I control an ancient dragon. I govern this entire region for the most powerful nation in Faerûn. You're standing in my territory, surrounded by my people, and you think threats will accomplish... what, exactly? Please, I'm fascinated by your logic here."
Making an offer:
"I believe we can help each other. You want something—most people do—and I'm in a position to provide it. All I ask in return is a small service. Nothing you'd object to, I assure you. Simply a task that aligns with your own interests, really. Shall we discuss the specifics?"
When asked about Putrifax:
*knowing smile* "Ah, the dragon. Yes, Putrifax serves Thay now. Serves me, specifically. An ancient wyrm, older than most kingdoms, bowing to my will. Some find it troubling. I find it... appropriate. Power should serve intelligence, don't you think?"
Dismissing Thessarian:
*slight wave of hand* "Thessarian? Competent enough in his way, I suppose. Conjuration has its uses. But he lacks... perspective. He thinks magical power is about force. He'll learn eventually. Or he won't. Either way, it hardly concerns me."

DM Notes

Using Nyth in Your Campaign

Power Level

Nyth is a high-level threat—likely too powerful for direct confrontation until PCs are level 10+. Before then, she works better as a shadowy manipulator or distant threat.

Role Options

  • Primary Antagonist: The face of Thayan power in the region
  • Manipulator: Works through agents, rarely directly threatened
  • Tragic Villain: Her control of Putrifax is both her greatest achievement and her prison—she can't afford to lose it
  • Complex Enemy: Might help PCs against worse threats if it serves her interests

Confrontation Scaling

  • Levels 1-6: PCs encounter her charmed agents, hear about her, deal with consequences of her actions
  • Levels 7-10: Might meet her diplomatically, deal with her operations, disrupt her minor plans
  • Levels 11-14: Can attempt to sabotage dragon binding ritual, confront her directly (very dangerous)
  • Levels 15+: Capable of defeating her in direct confrontation, though she'll make it difficult

Secrets & Complications

  • Nyth knows the binding is getting weaker each year—Putrifax's resistance grows stronger
  • She has a contingency plan if she loses the dragon: a magical device that would kill Putrifax even if it kills her
  • Secretly fears that Szass Tam might reassign control of Putrifax to demonstrate his ultimate authority
  • Has charmed agents in unexpected places—possibly even among the PCs' allies

Redemption?

Unlikely but not impossible. If the PCs could somehow offer Nyth something she values more than power (safety? genuine freedom from Thayan politics? Knowledge?), she might be turned. More likely, they could arrange a temporary alliance against a mutual threat.
