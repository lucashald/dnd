Putrifax, the Stone-Scarred Wyrm

Ancient Black Dragon of the Giantspire Mountains

Physical Description

Putrifax is an ancient black dragon of immense size, her scales bearing the distinctive scarring of centuries spent in limestone caverns. Where most black dragons have glossy, obsidian-like scales, hers are dulled and pitted from contact with the alkaline stone that both imprisons and weakens her. Her eyes burn with sulfurous yellow fire, and acid constantly drips from her fangs, though her breath weapon has been notably weakened by prolonged exposure to the life-magic infused limestone.

The dragon's most distinctive feature is the network of silver scars across her hide where binding runes have been carved and re-carved over the centuries. These mystical wounds never fully heal, serving as permanent anchor points for the geas spells that control her.

The Ancient Past (c. 800-1000 DR)

The Terror of the Northern Reaches

In her prime, Putrifax controlled a vast territory stretching from the Giantspire Mountains to the shores of the Moonsea. She was known as "the Acid Death" among the barbarian tribes, who spoke her name only in whispers. Her lair was a massive complex of natural caves she had expanded through centuries of acidic breath, creating a labyrinthine domain filled with the bones of her enemies and the treasures of a dozen conquered kingdoms.

The Druidic Alliance

Putrifax's downfall began when her predations threatened the sacred grove-cities of an ancient druidic civilization (predecessors to the builders of the Stone Forest). These druids had discovered that concentrated life-magic, channeled through limestone formations, could neutralize the dragon's acidic powers and weaken her natural magical resistances.

The First Binding (997 DR)

Led by the Archdruid Selenthe Moonwhisper (ancestor of the current Harper agent "Moonwhisper"), a coalition of druids, rangers, and barbarian shamans lured Putrifax to the Stone Forest during a winter solstice. Using the enhanced magical properties of the limestone karst and the seasonal alignment of natural forces, they managed to bind the dragon in the caves beneath the formation.

The ritual required immense sacrifice - Selenthe and twelve other druids gave their lives to power the binding spell, their spirits becoming part of the stone itself. This is why the limestone formations still radiate life-magic and why acid has reduced effectiveness in the area.

The Long Sleep

For nearly 500 years, Putrifax remained dormant in her stone prison, gradually weakening as the life-magic seeped into her being. The druidic civilization eventually declined and was conquered by the expanding Narfell Empire, but the binding held, maintained by the druids' sacrificed spirits.

The Thayan Discovery (1456 DR)

Rediscovering the Legend

Thayan scholars researching ancient binding rituals discovered references to the "Acid Death imprisoned in living stone" in captured Narfell texts. A expedition led by the young Red Wizard Nyth (now Tharchion) located the Stone Forest and found the weakened dragon.

Turning Prison into Tool

Rather than free Putrifax, the Thayans realized they could exploit the existing binding magic. By layering their own geas spells over the ancient druidic bindings, they could control the dragon while using the limestone's natural properties to make her more manageable.

The Annual Ritual

The Thayans discovered that their control magic degraded over time, requiring annual renewal. The winter solstice provides the optimal conditions for re-establishing the geas, as the natural magical forces that originally bound the dragon are at their strongest.

Three Decades of Servitude

For 36 years, Putrifax has served as Thay's northern enforcer. Her missions have included:

  • Terrorizing barbarian tribes into submission
  • Destroying rival wizard towers and sanctuaries
  • Intimidating diplomatic negotiations in Thay's favor
  • Serving as mobile siege weapon against fortified positions
  • Gathering intelligence through fear-induced interrogations

Current Mental State

Ancient Fury

Putrifax remembers everything from her centuries of freedom and her current imprisonment. Her hatred for her captors burns with the intensity of molten sulfur, but it's focused primarily on the Thayans rather than humanity in general. She views the Red Wizards as arrogant usurpers who corrupted sacred magic for their own ends.

Respect for Worthy Enemies

The dragon holds a grudging respect for the druids who originally bound her, seeing their sacrifice as honorable (if infuriating). This attitude extends to others who show similar courage and self-sacrifice in service of something greater than themselves.

Accumulated Wisdom

Centuries of forced servitude have given Putrifax unique insight into Thayan operations, magical theory, and regional politics. She has been forced to witness three decades of Red Wizard scheming and knows many of their secrets.

Weakened but Dangerous

While the limestone binding has reduced her power, Putrifax remains an ancient dragon with vast magical knowledge, tactical cunning, and burning motivation for revenge. She is physically weaker than a typical ancient black dragon but possibly more dangerous due to her desperate fury.

Plot Hooks: Dealing with the Freed Dragon

Option 1: The Vengeful Ally

Scenario: Putrifax focuses her initial fury on the Red Wizards who have controlled her, viewing the PCs as convenient tools for revenge.

Approach: The dragon offers a temporary alliance against Thay, providing intelligence about their operations and magical support in exchange for help hunting down specific Red Wizards.

Advantages:

  • Powerful magical ally with intimate knowledge of enemy operations
  • Dragon's mobility allows strikes against distant Thayan targets
  • Intelligence value is enormous - she knows Thayan secrets, safe houses, and future plans

Challenges:

  • Alliance is purely temporary and based on mutual benefit
  • Dragon's methods are ruthlessly violent and may conflict with PC morals
  • Other factions may view PCs as dragon-collaborators
  • Putrifax will eventually turn to her own agenda once revenge is satisfied

Key NPCs:

  • Thessarian Vekt: The Red Wizard whose journal the PCs found - primary target for dragon revenge
  • Tharchion Nyth: Current binder and main architect of dragon's imprisonment

Option 2: The Negotiated Peace

Scenario: PCs attempt to broker a deal that satisfies the dragon's need for revenge while protecting innocent lives.

Approach: Diplomatic negotiations involving territorial concessions, tribute, or specific targets for the dragon's wrath.

Potential Terms:

  • Dragon gets specific revenge against her primary captors
  • Territorial agreement - Putrifax claims a remote area as her new domain
  • Information exchange - dragon provides intelligence in return for certain freedoms
  • Time limit - agreement lasts for specific period while longer-term solution is found

Advantages:

  • Prevents immediate widespread destruction
  • Allows time to develop longer-term solutions
  • Could establish the dragon as a neutral party in regional politics
  • PCs gain reputation as master diplomats

Challenges:

  • Dragons are not known for keeping agreements with "lesser beings"
  • Other factions may not respect any deal made with an ancient black dragon
  • Enforcement of any agreement is problematic
  • Dragon's fundamental nature conflicts with civilized society

Option 3: The Spiritual Solution

Scenario: PCs discover the connection between Putrifax and the original druidic binding, leading to a supernatural resolution.

Approach: Communication with the spirits of the original druids who sacrificed themselves, potentially leading to a new form of binding or spiritual transformation.

Possible Outcomes:

  • Redemption Arc: Dragon's exposure to life-magic begins spiritual transformation
  • Spiritual Binding: New agreement with druidic spirits creates different form of containment
  • Reincarnation: Dragon's spirit is transferred to a less destructive form
  • Sacred Guardian: Transformation into protector of natural sites rather than destroyer

Advantages:

  • Addresses root cause rather than just symptoms
  • Honors the original druids' sacrifice
  • Could create a genuinely reformed ally
  • Thematically satisfying resolution to ancient magical working

Challenges:

  • Requires significant magical research and high-level spell work
  • Dragon must be willing participant for most versions
  • May require additional sacrifices from PCs or allies
  • Uncertain outcome - ancient magic is unpredictable

Option 4: The Tactical Elimination

Scenario: PCs determine the dragon cannot be reasoned with or contained, requiring direct action.

Approach: Strategic assassination or magical banishment using the dragon's weakened state and the site's natural advantages.

Methods:

  • Assassination: Strike while dragon is still weakened from binding
  • Re-binding: Attempt to restore or strengthen the original druidic binding
  • Banishment: Use planar magic to send dragon to another realm
  • Dragonslaying: Traditional kill-the-dragon approach with environmental advantages

Advantages:

  • Permanent solution to the dragon threat
  • Prevents any possibility of future revenge against innocents
  • Establishes PCs as legendary dragonslayers
  • Removes major destabilizing force from regional politics

Challenges:

  • Even weakened, an ancient dragon is an extremely dangerous opponent
  • Goes against Harper philosophy of seeking alternatives to violence
  • May anger other draconic beings or dragon-allied factions
  • Success requires careful planning and significant resources

Option 5: The Knowledge Seeker

Scenario: Putrifax becomes a source of ancient knowledge and magical lore rather than an active threat.

Approach: Establish the dragon as an oracle or magical consultant, contained but not controlled.

Implementation:

  • Modified binding that prevents violence but allows communication
  • Dragon exchanges information for specific services or tributes
  • Becomes a neutral source of magical knowledge for multiple factions
  • PCs serve as intermediaries between dragon and various clients

Advantages:

  • Utilizes dragon's vast knowledge and magical expertise
  • Creates ongoing plot opportunities and NPC interactions
  • Dragon becomes invested in regional stability as source of tribute
  • Allows for character growth as PCs learn from ancient perspective

Challenges:

  • Requires trust that dragon will honor limitations
  • Other factions may try to exploit or eliminate the arrangement
  • Dragon's advice may serve her own hidden agenda
  • Maintaining the balance requires ongoing PC attention

Putrifax's Likely Priorities Upon Freedom

Immediate (First 24-48 Hours)

  1. Eliminate Immediate Threats: Attack any Red Wizards or Thayan soldiers in the area
  1. Reclaim Personal Items: Recover any treasure or artifacts taken from her original hoard
  1. Test Her Power: Determine how much the binding has weakened her abilities
  1. Secure Territory: Find a defensible location for recovery and planning

Short Term (First Month)

  1. Targeted Revenge: Hunt down specific individuals responsible for her captivity
  1. Intelligence Gathering: Assess current political and magical landscape
  1. Recovery: Regain strength and magical power through rest and feeding
  1. Territory Establishment: Claim a new domain, possibly in familiar mountain regions

Long Term (Ongoing)

  1. Complete Revenge: Systematic elimination of Thayan leadership
  1. Power Restoration: Rebuild magical strength and treasure hoard
  1. Political Maneuvering: Establish position in regional power structure
  1. Legacy Planning: Ensure her freedom can never again be compromised

Dragon's Resources and Capabilities

Knowledge Assets

  • Intimate details of Thayan military and magical operations
  • Location of hidden Thayan bases and safe houses
  • Identity of Thayan agents throughout the northern regions
  • Ancient magical lore from centuries of experience
  • Geographic knowledge of remote locations and secret passages

Magical Capabilities (Current)

  • Reduced but still formidable spellcasting ability
  • Weakened acid breath (still dangerous to most opponents)
  • Flight and mobility advantages
  • Natural dragon magic resistance and immunities
  • Centuries of accumulated magical items and knowledge

Political Leverage

  • Information valuable to multiple factions
  • Symbol of successful resistance to Thayan expansion
  • Potential rallying point for anti-Thayan forces
  • Living connection to ancient magical traditions

Motivation Factors

  • Burning desire for revenge against specific individuals
  • Pride in her ancient status and power
  • Respect for courage and magical competence
  • Disdain for weakness and cowardice
  • Pragmatic recognition of current limitations
