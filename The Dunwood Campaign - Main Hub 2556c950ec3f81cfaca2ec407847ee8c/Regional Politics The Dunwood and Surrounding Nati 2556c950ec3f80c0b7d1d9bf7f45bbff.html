Regional Politics: The Dunwood and Surrounding Nations

As of 1492 DR

Geographic Context

The Dunwood sits in a politically fractured region of northeastern Faerûn, where the collapse of ancient empires has left a patchwork of small kingdoms, city-states, and wilderness territories. The area remains a frontier where civilization meets the untamed North.

Major Political Powers

Damara (Southwest)

Government: Kingdom under King Gareth Dragonsbane's legacy (ruled by his successors) Capital: Heliogabalus Relationship to Campaign: Closest major civilization, source of Harper support

Key Details:

  • Founded after the defeat of the Witch-King Zhengyi in 1359 DR
  • Relatively young kingdom still establishing firm borders
  • Strong tradition of heroism and monster-hunting
  • Uthmere is a major Damaran city and Harper operations base
  • Generally supportive of Harper activities as they help secure the frontier
  • Limited military presence in the far northeast due to other priorities

Current Politics:

  • Dealing with internal consolidation and economic development
  • Occasional conflicts with Vaasa to the east
  • Supportive of adventuring groups who help secure the borderlands
  • King's focus is on establishing trade routes and diplomatic relations

Vaasa (Southeast)

Government: Recovering kingdom, formerly the Witch-King's domain Capital: Palischuk (half-orc city) Relationship to Campaign: Distant but potentially significant

Key Details:

  • Still recovering from centuries of undead rule under Zhengyi
  • Unusual for having Palischuk, a legitimately governed half-orc city
  • Sparse population scattered across a blasted landscape
  • Many ruins and undead-tainted areas remain dangerous
  • Bloodstone Village marks the border region between Damara and Vaasa

Current Politics:

  • Struggling to establish legitimate government after the Witch-King's defeat
  • Frequent conflicts with Damara over border territories
  • Internal tensions between human and orc/half-orc populations
  • Potential ally for orc clans if approached diplomatically

Thay (Far Southwest)

Government: Magocracy ruled by Szass Tam and the Red Wizards Capital: Eltabbar Relationship to Campaign: Primary antagonist faction

Key Details:

  • Powerful magical nation with expansionist ambitions
  • Uses Putrifax as a strategic weapon for northern operations
  • Maintains secret bases and agents throughout the region
  • Views the northern territories as potential conquest targets
  • Excellent magical intelligence networks

Current Operations:

  • Annual dragon binding ritual represents major magical undertaking
  • Forward camps and staging areas established seasonally
  • Recruiting local agents and informants
  • Long-term goal likely involves conquering Damara and establishing northern puppet states

Political Motivation:

  • Szass Tam seeks to expand Thayan influence throughout Faerûn
  • Dragon control provides both military might and psychological warfare
  • Northern territories offer resources and strategic positioning against other powers

Regional Powers and City-States

The Silver Marches (Luruar) (West)

Status: Confederate alliance in decline after the War of the Silver Marches (1484-1485 DR) Key Cities: Silverymoon, Everlund, Sundabar

Current Situation:

  • Confederation dissolved following orc invasion of Many-Arrows
  • Individual cities now operate independently
  • Reduced influence in eastern territories like the Dunwood
  • Alustriel Silverhand and Storm Silverhand remain influential Harper leaders
  • Limited resources to project power eastward due to reconstruction needs

Impact on Campaign:

  • Source of high-level Harper operatives and magical support
  • Historical knowledge of dealing with Thayan expansion
  • Possible reinforcements if the Putrifax situation escalates dramatically

The Vast (Southwest)

Government: Loose collection of city-states and merchant princes Key Cities: Ravens Bluff, Procampur, Tantras

Relevance:

  • Major trade network that could be threatened by Thayan expansion
  • Source of mercenaries and adventuring companies
  • Economic interests in keeping northern trade routes secure
  • Limited direct political influence in Dunwood region

Impiltur (South)

Government: Ancient kingdom, recently restored Capital: Lyrabar

Current Status:

  • Recovering from centuries of internal strife and demon invasion
  • Limited military capacity for northern operations
  • Focused on internal rebuilding and securing southern borders
  • Potential long-term ally against Thayan expansion

Non-State Actors and Factions

Many-Arrows Orc Kingdom (Scattered)

Status: Defeated and dispersed after the War of the Silver Marches Relevance: Refugee populations and war-bands in northern mountains

Current Situation:

  • Kingdom officially dissolved, King Obould Many-Arrows' legacy ended
  • Scattered orc clans fled north and east into areas like Giantspire Mountains
  • Some clans have integrated peacefully, others remain hostile
  • Grimjaw's Hold represents the more pragmatic faction

Political Implications:

  • Potential allies against common threats (like dragon-controlling Thayans)
  • Internal divisions between traditionalist war-clans and pragmatic survivors
  • Some clans might be recruited by Thay as expendable troops
  • Harper policy generally supportive of peaceful integration

The Harpers (Network Organization)

Leadership: Storm Silverhand, Alustriel Silverhand, other senior agents Area of Operations: Throughout the North and beyond

Current Priorities:

  • Preventing Thayan expansion into the northern territories
  • Supporting legitimate governments and opposing tyranny
  • Protecting important magical sites and artifacts
  • Maintaining network of agents and safe houses

Operational Philosophy:

  • "Sometimes the enemy of evil is just a different kind of evil"
  • Willing to make pragmatic alliances for the greater good
  • Long-term thinking about regional stability
  • Balance between action and secrecy

Cult of the Dragon (Secret Organization)

Status: Weakened but still active in northern regions Relevance: Potential complication for dragon-related operations

Potential Involvement:

  • Interest in Putrifax as a potential dracolich candidate
  • May attempt to interfere with or exploit the binding ritual
  • Could provide alternative solution to the dragon problem
  • Represents a "third option" beyond Harper or Thayan control

Current Political Climate

Power Vacuum in the North

  • Collapse of Luruar left northeastern territories largely ungoverned
  • No major power currently claims sovereignty over Dunwood region
  • Creates opportunity for Thayan expansion and influence operations
  • Local settlements and clans operate with de facto independence

Post-War Recovery Period

  • War of the Silver Marches (1484-1485) ended only 7-8 years ago
  • Many traditional alliances and power structures still rebuilding
  • Opportunity for new players to establish influence
  • General wariness of large-scale military operations

Economic Considerations

  • Northern trade routes remain vulnerable to monster attacks and banditry
  • Merchant interests favor stability and security over political control
  • Dragon presence disrupts trade and threatens economic development
  • Successful Harper operation could improve regional commerce

Magical Politics

  • Thay's use of controlled dragon represents escalation in magical warfare
  • Other nations lack equivalent magical assets for deterrence
  • Harper network provides counterbalance to Thayan magical intelligence
  • Ancient magical sites like Stone Forest hold strategic importance

Campaign Implications

Political Support for Harper Operations

  • Damara: Quietly supportive, provides supplies and safe passage
  • Vaasa: Neutral to positive, especially if orc clans are treated fairly
  • Silver Marches: Strong support from leadership, limited material resources
  • Thay: Obviously hostile, will escalate if operation discovered

Consequences of Success

  • Reduced Thayan influence in northern territories
  • Improved relations with local orc clans
  • Strengthened Harper position in regional politics
  • Potential for expanded Harper operations in the area

Consequences of Failure

  • Thayan consolidation of dragon control
  • Possible expansion of Thayan operations northward
  • Loss of Harper credibility and regional influence
  • Increased threat to Damaran and other northern settlements

Wildcard Factors

  • Putrifax's own agenda once freed from magical control
  • Possible intervention by other dragon-interested factions
  • Unexpected alliances between former enemies
  • Discovery of ancient magical secrets that change the power balance
