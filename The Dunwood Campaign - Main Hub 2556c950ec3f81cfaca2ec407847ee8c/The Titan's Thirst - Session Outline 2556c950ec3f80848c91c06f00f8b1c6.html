The Titan's Thirst - Session Outline

A creature from the cursed glacial pool threatens the valley

Session Overview

Level: 5-7

Duration: 4-6 hours

Theme: Ancient curses, environmental horror, community defense

A massive creature, transformed by drinking from the legendary Cursed Pool of Jotunn's Tears, descends from the high peaks to threaten Fjord's End. The PCs must decide whether to defend the village, investigate the source of the curse, or attempt to cure the afflicted beast.

Opening: The Warning Signs

Scene 1: Unnatural Omens (15 minutes)

Location: Fjord's End village

Time: Early morning, mist rising from the fjord

Read-Aloud:

The morning mist clings to the fjord's dark waters as you emerge from the Glacier's Rest Inn. But something feels wrong. The usual symphony of mountain birds is absent, replaced by an oppressive silence that seems to press against your ears. Magnus Ironhold stands at the harbor's edge, his weathered face creased with worry as he stares up at the Giantspire peaks.

Key Events:

  • Missing Wildlife: No birds, and the ravens that usually perch around the village have vanished
  • Nervous Animals: Village dogs whine and refuse to leave their owners' sides
  • Geological Signs: Minor tremors felt through the ground, loose stones falling from cliff faces
  • Magnus's Concern: The dwarf speaks of "old warnings" and "things that should stay sleeping"

Information Gathering:

  • Magnus Ironhold knows legends of the Cursed Pool but dismisses them as "mountain tales"
  • Tormund Stormwatch reports strange lights on the high peaks three nights ago
  • Captain Brenna mentions her boats' fish-finding equipment detected something massive moving in the deep fjord waters

Scene 2: The Titan's Roar (10 minutes)

Location: Fjord's End

Time: Mid-morning

Read-Aloud:

A sound unlike anything you've ever heard echoes through the fjord—a roar that seems to shake the very foundations of the mountains. It's not the cry of any natural beast, but something deeper, more primal, touched with an anguish that makes your bones ache. In the distance, high on the mountain slopes, you see a cascade of rocks tumbling down the cliffs. Something vast is moving up there, and it's heading this way.

Key Events:

  • The First Roar: Establishes the creature's presence and emotional state (pain/rage)
  • Avalanche Activity: Rocks and snow falling from the upper slopes
  • Panic Preparations: Villagers begin emergency preparations based on old protocols
  • Distance Assessment: The creature is still hours away but clearly approaching

NPC Reactions:

  • Magnus immediately begins organizing village defenses
  • Kira Brightwater starts moving supplies to secure locations
  • Tormund prepares to scout the creature's approach
  • Some villagers want to flee by boat, others insist on defending their homes

Act I: Investigation and Preparation

Scene 3: Choosing a Response (20 minutes)

Location: Village center

Time: Late morning

The villagers look to the PCs for leadership. Multiple options are presented:

Option A: Direct Defense

  • Fortify the village and prepare for siege
  • Organize evacuation of non-combatants
  • Set up defensive positions using terrain advantages

Option B: Intercept the Threat

  • Travel up the mountain to meet the creature before it reaches the village
  • Attempt to drive it away or kill it in more favorable terrain
  • Risk being caught in the open against an unknown threat

Option C: Investigate the Source

  • Seek out the Cursed Pool to understand what happened
  • Possibly find a way to reverse the transformation
  • Leave the village temporarily vulnerable

Option D: Diplomatic Solution

  • Attempt to communicate with the creature
  • Discover if intelligence remains beneath the curse
  • Potentially the most dangerous but most rewarding approach

Key NPCs Present:

  • Magnus Ironhold: Favors fortification and defense
  • Captain Brenna: Advocates for partial evacuation by boat
  • Tormund Stormwatch: Volunteers to guide any mountain expedition
  • Kira Brightwater: Supports investigating the root cause

Scene 4: Gathering Resources (30 minutes)

Location: Various village locations

Time: Midday

Based on the party's chosen approach, they can gather resources and information:

If Defending:

  • Magnus's Arsenal: Old weapons and armor from his adventuring days
  • Defensive Positions: Survey the village's tactical advantages
  • Evacuation Routes: Plan fallback positions and escape paths
  • Village Militia: Organize and arm willing defenders

If Investigating/Intercepting:

  • Mountain Gear: Cold weather equipment, climbing gear, alpine supplies
  • Local Knowledge: Tormund provides maps and route information
  • Legend Research: Magnus shares what he knows about the Cursed Pool
  • Magical Preparation: Kira has some minor magical items and healing supplies

Important Information Available:

  • The Cursed Pool Legend: High glacial tarn touched by ancient giant magic
  • Location: Two days' climb up the northern slopes, near the glacier's edge
  • Previous Incidents: Rumors of enlarged animals, but nothing this massive
  • The Curse Nature: Transformation seems permanent but might be reversible

Act II: The Encounter

Scene 5A: The Village Defense (If PCs chose to fortify)

The Creature Arrives (45 minutes)

Location: Fjord's End and surrounding area

Time: Late afternoon

Read-Aloud:

The ground trembles with each footstep as the creature finally comes into view around the mountain's shoulder. What was once a brown bear now stands thirty feet tall at the shoulder, its fur matted with ice and its eyes glowing with an unnatural blue light. Ancient ice clings to its massive claws, and with each breath, clouds of freezing mist pour from its muzzle. It moves with the slow, inevitable purpose of a glacier—unstoppable and inexorable.

Titan Bear Stats (CR 8):

  • Gargantuan Beast (originally Brown Bear)
  • AC: 16 (Natural Armor)
  • HP: 180 (19d12 + 57)
  • Speed: 40 ft., climb 30 ft.
  • STR: 25, DEX: 10, CON: 16, INT: 4, WIS: 13, CHA: 7
  • Damage Resistances: Cold
  • Senses: Darkvision 60 ft.
  • Challenge: 8 (3,900 XP)

Special Abilities:

  • Glacial Breath: (Recharge 5-6) 30-foot cone, 3d8 cold damage, DEX save DC 15
  • Trampling Charge: If moves 20+ feet straight toward target, extra 2d10 bludgeoning damage
  • Ice Aura: 10-foot radius difficult terrain from forming ice
  • Legendary Actions: 3 per turn (move, claw attack, or breath weapon)

Encounter Phases:

  1. Approach: Creature destroys outer defenses, villagers flee to prepared positions
  1. Initial Assault: Bear attacks fortified positions, PCs coordinate defense
  1. Desperate Defense: Bear breaks through, combat moves to village center
  1. Resolution: Defeat, drive off, or cure the creature

Scene 5B: Mountain Interception (If PCs chose to intercept)

The High Encounter (45 minutes)

Location: Mountain slopes above Fjord's End

Time: Afternoon, depending on travel time

Environmental Challenges:

  • Treacherous Terrain: Steep slopes, loose rock, patches of ice
  • Altitude Effects: Thin air, cold temperatures, sudden weather changes
  • Limited Escape Routes: Narrow ledges, cliff faces, avalanche danger

Tactical Advantages:

  • Higher Ground: PCs can choose engagement distance and angle
  • Environmental Weapons: Rockslides, ice formations, narrow passages
  • Retreat Options: Multiple paths to fall back if needed

The Encounter: Same Titan Bear statistics, but modified tactics:

  • Uses terrain to its advantage (climbing, jumping between ledges)
  • Glacial breath more dangerous in confined spaces
  • Avalanche risk from loud sounds or impacts
  • Potential to drive creature off rather than kill it

Scene 5C: The Cursed Pool (If PCs chose investigation)

The Source of Corruption (60 minutes)

Location: High glacial cirque, Cursed Pool of Jotunn's Tears

Time: After mountain travel

Read-Aloud:

The glacial pool stretches before you like a mirror of black ice, its surface so still it might be solid. But you can see the faint shimmer of movement beneath, and the water itself seems to pulse with a malevolent blue light. Ancient runes are carved into the surrounding stone—giant script that predates any human settlement. The very air here thrums with old magic, and you can feel the weight of ancient sorrow pressing down upon you.

The Pool's Properties:

  • Curse Detection: Powerful necromantic and transmutation magic
  • Giant Runes: Tell the story of a giant's grief and revenge
  • Active Magic: Pool still transforms creatures that drink from it
  • Purification Possibility: High-level magic or specific ritual components could cleanse it

Potential Encounters:

  • Enlarged Mountain Goats: 2-3 Large-sized goats with Titan Bear template (scaled down)
  • Frost Giant Spirit: The source of the curse, bound to the pool
  • Environmental Hazards: Unstable ice, magical storms, altitude sickness

Resolution Options:

  • Cleanse the Pool: Remove the curse permanently (requires specific magic/components)
  • Temporarily Suppress: Stop current effects but curse remains dormant
  • Bind the Spirit: Negotiate with the giant's ghost to end its revenge
  • Gather Information: Learn how to reverse transformations

Act III: Resolution and Consequences

Scene 6: Aftermath (30 minutes)

Location: Varies based on previous choices

Time: Evening

If Creature Defeated:

  • Village celebration, but somber recognition of the ongoing threat
  • Examination of creature remains reveals extent of magical transformation
  • Discussion of long-term solutions to prevent future incidents

If Creature Driven Off:

  • Temporary relief, but knowledge that threat could return
  • Debate about pursuing permanent solution vs. improved defenses
  • Tracking the creature's retreat to determine its new territory

If Curse Resolved:

  • Creature returns to normal size (if still alive) and flees
  • Pool's purification has regional effects (better weather, wildlife returns)
  • Villagers' gratitude and recognition of PCs as true heroes

If Investigation Only:

  • PCs return with knowledge but threat still active
  • Must choose immediate response with better information
  • Opportunity for more informed second attempt

Scene 7: Long-term Planning (15 minutes)

Location: Fjord's End village center

Time: That evening

Village Council Meeting:

  • Magnus Ironhold: Proposes establishing formal watch on the high peaks
  • Captain Brenna: Suggests creating evacuation protocols for similar threats
  • Tormund Stormwatch: Volunteers to monitor the mountain approach routes
  • Kira Brightwater: Advocates for researching other local legends and threats

PC Recognition:

  • Village formally recognizes PCs as protectors and heroes
  • Offers of permanent residence, honorary citizenship, or other rewards
  • Access to village resources for future adventures
  • Introduction to regional contacts and allies

Future Hooks:

  • Other cursed locations mentioned in giant lore
  • Red Wizard interest in the pool's transformative magic
  • Requests for help from other communities facing similar threats
  • Investigation into who or what originally cursed the pool

Alternative Developments

Diplomatic Resolution

If PCs attempt to communicate with the Titan Bear:

  • Intelligence Check (DC 15): Recognize signs of remaining animal intelligence
  • Animal Handling (DC 20): Calm the creature enough for interaction
  • Druidcraft/Speak with Animals: Discover the bear's pain and confusion
  • Possible Outcomes: Lead creature back to pool for healing, establish ongoing relationship

Research Discovery

Investigation into the pool's history might reveal:

  • Giant's Tragedy: Pool created by giant parent's grief over lost child
  • Reversal Method: Specific ritual requiring rare components
  • Temporal Aspects: Curse grows stronger during certain celestial alignments
  • Other Victims: Maps to locations of other transformed creatures

Escalation Scenarios

If the session runs long or PCs need additional challenges:

  • Multiple Creatures: Family group of transformed animals arrives
  • Environmental Disaster: Creature's presence triggers avalanche or rockfall
  • Outside Interest: Red Wizard agents arrive to study/capture the creature
  • Chain Reaction: Creature's roar attracts other dangerous mountain predators

Rewards and Experience

Combat Victory

  • Titan Bear: 3,900 XP (divided among party)
  • Environmental Challenges: 200-500 XP per overcome obstacle
  • Tactical Bonus: Extra XP for creative use of terrain or teamwork

Investigation Success

  • Pool Discovery: 1,000 XP for finding and understanding the source
  • Lore Uncovered: 500 XP for learning the pool's history
  • Curse Resolution: 2,000 XP for permanently solving the problem

Social/Roleplay

  • Village Relations: 500 XP for positive community outcome
  • Creative Solutions: 300-700 XP for non-combat problem solving
  • Future Planning: 200 XP for establishing long-term protections

Material Rewards

  • Village Gratitude: Discounted services, free lodging, equipment access
  • Giant Relics: Ancient items from the pool site (if discovered)
  • Magical Components: Materials from transformed creature (if defeated)
  • Maps and Information: Details about other regional threats and opportunities

Session Notes for DM

Flexibility Points

  • Creature's approach speed can be adjusted based on party preparation time needed
  • Pool location can be relocated if PCs choose unexpected travel route
  • Creature's intelligence level can vary based on party's attempted interaction
  • Village NPC reactions can be modified based on established relationships

Scaling Options

  • Easier: Reduce creature HP to 120, remove legendary actions
  • Harder: Add second transformed creature or environmental hazards
  • Roleplay Focus: Emphasize investigation and negotiation over combat
  • Combat Focus: Add village defense mini-encounters with militia

Connection to Main Campaign

  • Harper Interest: Ancient magic sites relevant to larger supernatural threats
  • Red Wizard Connection: Transformation magic could interest Thayan researchers
  • Regional Stability: Success increases village security and PC reputation
  • Supernatural Escalation: Part of pattern of increasing magical threats in region

This session provides a complete adventure that can stand alone or integrate seamlessly into the larger Dunwood campaign, offering multiple resolution paths while highlighting the region's ancient magical dangers.
