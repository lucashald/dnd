The Silver Marches (Former Luruar Confederation)

Overview

The Silver Marches, formerly known as the confederation of Luruar, represents one of the most significant political transformations in northeastern Faerûn's recent history. Once a powerful alliance of cities and strongholds under the leadership of Alustriel Silverhand, the confederation was devastated by the War of the Silver Marches (1484-1485 DR) and formally disbanded in 1488 DR, leaving its former member cities as independent entities struggling with recovery and isolation.

Current Political Status (1492 DR)

  • Government: Dissolved confederation; individual city-states operate independently
  • Former Leadership: Alustriel Silverhand (now focused on Harper activities)
  • Political System: Each former member city now governs itself
  • Military Status: Fragmented defense, no unified command structure
  • Regional Influence: Greatly diminished from pre-war levels

Historical Context: The War of the Silver Marches

The confederation's destruction was orchestrated by drow from Menzoberranzan under Lolth's orders. The conflict saw the Kingdom of Many-Arrows orcs, led by warlord Hartusk, coordinate with frost giants and white dragons Arauthator and Aurbangras to assault major settlements. The war's devastation was comprehensive and included:

  • Nesmé: Completely destroyed and remains uninhabited ruins
  • Sundabar: Conquered and renamed "Hartusk Keep" with entire surface population eliminated
  • Major Dwarfholds: Citadel Adbar, Citadel Felbarr, and Mithral Hall all faced extended sieges
  • Silverymoon: Survived siege but suffered significant damage and population loss

Current Status of Former Member Cities

Silverymoon

  • Status: Independent city-state, severely weakened
  • Leadership: Recovering from war damage
  • Population: Reduced significantly from pre-war levels
  • Influence: Limited to immediate surrounding area

Sundabar

  • Status: Completely withdrawn to underground Everfire caverns
  • Population: Purely dwarven, extremely isolationist
  • Leadership: Dwarven council focused on survival
  • External Relations: Minimal contact with surface world

Everlund

  • Status: Independent city struggling with recovery
  • Focus: Rebuilding infrastructure and trade relationships
  • Military: Limited to local defense forces
  • Trade: Attempting to reestablish merchant connections

Dwarfholds (Citadel Adbar, Citadel Felbarr, Mithral Hall)

  • Status: Independent strongholds focused on internal rebuilding
  • Leadership: Traditional clan-based governance
  • Cooperation: Minimal coordination between holds
  • Defense: Each maintains separate military forces

Political Fragmentation & Consequences

The League of Silver Marches formally disbanded in 1488 DR due to post-war disagreements over mutual support failures during the crisis. This political fragmentation has eliminated the unified defense structure that once protected the North, leaving individual settlements vulnerable to future threats and creating opportunities for external powers to exploit the instability.

Regional Impact & Power Vacuum

The confederation's collapse has created a significant power vacuum in northwestern Faerûn:

  • No unified response capability to regional threats
  • Trade routes disrupted and unprotected
  • Individual cities lack resources for major defensive operations
  • External powers (like Thay) can exploit weakened defenses

Relations with Other Powers

  • Harpers: Storm Silverhand and Alustriel Silverhand remain influential leaders, though resources are limited
  • Damara: Supportive relationships but limited material assistance available
  • Thay: Opportunity for increased Red Wizard infiltration and influence
  • Regional Threats: Unable to coordinate responses to dangers like dragon activity

Harper Connections

Despite the confederation's collapse, the Silver Marches region remains crucial to Harper operations:

  • Leadership: Alustriel and Storm Silverhand continue Harper activities
  • Intelligence Network: Maintains agents throughout former confederation territories
  • Strategic Knowledge: Extensive experience dealing with regional magical threats
  • Resources: Limited material support but valuable expertise and coordination

Campaign Relevance

  • Represents recent catastrophic failure of regional cooperation
  • Individual cities may be more willing to accept external assistance
  • Weakened state creates opportunities for Harper recruitment and operations
  • Former confederation leaders possess valuable intelligence about regional threats
  • Political fragmentation provides cover for covert operations
  • Recovery efforts may offer legitimate reasons for Harper presence
  • Demonstrates consequences of failing to address major threats cooperatively
