Uthmere

Quick Reference

Type: Trading Town Population: ~250 Political Control: Independent (Merchant Council) Region: Northern Trading Corridor Threat Level: Low

Location Overview

Uthmere is a prosperous trading town located along the northern trading corridors, known for its merchant-friendly policies and neutral stance on regional conflicts. The town has earned a reputation as a reliable hub for commerce, where deals are made fairly and disputes are settled through negotiation rather than force. Unlike Ilmwatch's military focus, Uthmere thrives on its reputation for honesty and good business practices.

Geography & Setting

  • Location: Northern trading corridor, positioned at a natural crossroads
  • Terrain: Gentle slopes with accessible roads, surrounded by moderate forests
  • Fortifications: Low stone walls and wooden gates—practical rather than military-grade
  • Buildings: Merchant houses, trading warehouses, guild halls, market square, inn, craftsperson workshops
  • Market: Well-organized market district with permanent merchant stalls
  • Trade: Major intersection for north-south and east-west trade routes

Government & Leadership

Lord Uthlain

Title: Lord of Uthmere

Lord Uthlain is the effective ruler of Uthmere, governing through a system of appointed officials known as the "lordlain" (literally "servants of the lord"). His governance is generally fair and orderly, prioritizing commercial prosperity and public safety. However, he maintains absolute authority—those who "get too highnosed" or presume to speak out against his rule often find that unfortunate circumstances befall them. Uthmere prospers under his leadership, and most citizens accept his rule without complaint, preferring his practical authority to the instability and chaos of other settlements.

The Lordlain (City Officials)

The lordlain are appointed administrators who handle the day-to-day governance of Uthmere under Lord Uthlain's authority. They maintain the census (the "Lord's ledger"), collect taxes, oversee markets, and manage public services. They are careful to distinguish between "Uthmaar" (proper citizens on the Lord's ledger who pay taxes) and "outlanders" (temporary residents or those operating without proper registration).

The Cudgels

The Cudgels are Uthmere's enforcement force—a professional militia who maintain order in the town. They enforce Lord Uthlain's laws with consistency and are known for their pragmatism. While they maintain order firmly, they are not oppressive so long as citizens abide by local law. The Cudgels are particularly zealous about enforcing Uthmere's immigration policies, especially concerning non-human residents; any non-human sailor who attempts to settle in Uthmere will be "called on" by the Cudgels and ordered to depart.

Guilds & Organizations

  • Merchant's Guild: Primary organization governing trade, tariffs, and commercial regulations
  • Craftsperson's Union: Oversees quality standards for goods produced in town
  • Guild Arbitration Office: Neutral body for settling commercial disputes

Key NPCs

Aldric Thorne - Curio Merchant & Harper Contact

Race: Human

Age: Mid-60s, retired from adventuring

Role: Owner of the Curiosities & Antiquities shop; informal Harper safe house keeper

Affiliation: The Harpers (affiliate, covert supporter)

Background: Former adventurer and merchant who settled in Uthmere after years of traveling. His experiences have given him a broad network of contacts and deep appreciation for Harper ideals of freedom and justice.

Connection: Primary contact for Ingrid Runestone in Uthmere; provides supplies, safe lodging, and message relay services for traveling agents

Specialty: Sourcing rare items, providing safe passage for travelers, information brokering, contacts with merchant networks

Personality: Warm, grandfatherly demeanor masks a sharp mind and keen eye for detail. He treats his shop and safe house with care, and is fiercely protective of those who come to him through proper Harper channels.

The Shop: "Curiosities & Antiquities" serves as both genuine business and intelligence clearinghouse. His inventory includes actual rare items alongside supplies that might be needed by traveling agents. A hidden room in the back can shelter refugees or serve as a secure meeting place.

Councillor Helena Vross - Merchant Council Member

Race: Dwarf

Role: Senior council member; head of merchant regulations

Specialty: Trade agreements, merchant relations, quality standards

Personality: Stern but fair businesswoman. Maintains strict ethical standards for town commerce. Not a Harper affiliate, but shares values of fairness and neutrality that Harper agents appreciate.

Thomas "Tom" Greenbottle - Inn Proprietor

Race: Halfling

Role: Owner and operator of the Wayfarers' Rest Inn

Specialty: Hospitality, gossip (he knows everything that happens in town), reliable lodging

Reputation: Known for excellent food, clean rooms, and discretion. Tom is a reliable source of information about traveling merchants and their business dealings.

Elise Cordova - Guard Captain

Race: Half-Elf

Role: Captain of the town guard and militia

Specialty: Town security, road safety, caravan escort coordination

Personality: Professional and capable. Maintains good relationships with merchants and travelers. Will cooperate with reasonable requests from Harper agents if they don't compromise town security.

Facilities & Services

  • The Wayfarers' Rest Inn: Quality lodging and dining; excellent information source through Tom Greenbottle
  • Curiosities & Antiquities: Curio shop and safe house run by Aldric Thorne; source for rare supplies and items
  • Market Square: Open-air market with permanent merchant stalls; daily trading and commerce
  • Guild Halls: Various guild halls open to merchants and craftspeople seeking services
  • Craftsperson District: Blacksmiths, armorers, leatherworkers, and other craftspeople available for custom work
  • Stables & Caravansary: Professional animal care and caravan staging area
  • Message Relay: Guild-maintained courier service connecting to major trade routes

Atmosphere & Character

Normal Conditions

  • Bustling market activity, especially during market days
  • Constant flow of merchants and caravans passing through
  • Professional guard presence (the Cudgels) maintaining order without oppression
  • Atmosphere of cooperation and fair dealing among the merchant classes
  • Diverse population primarily from human stock; most other races tolerated but discouraged from settling
  • A sense of prosperity and confidence among the Uthmaar

Uthmere's Character

Uthmere is often called "a place that has all anyone needs"—it serves as a supply center for the entire region, offering superb tools, quality textiles, reliable equipment, provisions, and more. The town prospers on the philosophy that "if an Uth shop doesn't have it, either a trader will visit with it next season, or an Uth dealer will get it for you." Citizens and visitors alike appreciate the clean streets, solid buildings, reliable security, and fair dealing—a striking contrast to the chaos and squalor of larger cities.

Regional Tensions

The Great Dale Conflict: Uthmere's expansion and commercial ambition has created significant tension with the rest of the Great Dale region. The druidic Circle of Leth and its allies view Uthmere as a threat to the natural world, seeing Lord Uthlain and his ambitions as fundamentally opposed to the preservation of the wilderness. Rumors spread by these groups characterize Uthmere's merchants as "coin-rakers" and "despoilers" intent on seizing and destroying the Great Dale for profit.

Circle of Leth Opposition: Members of the Circle actively work against Uthmere's interests, encouraging settlement preservation and opposing trade expansion. Druids and their allies have been known to interfere with merchants, take direct action against Uthmere operations, and sabotage expeditions into the surrounding wilderness.

Town Philosophy

Uthmere operates on principles of fair dealing, neutral trade, and practical governance. The town welcomes travelers and merchants of all backgrounds, provided they respect local law and custom. This philosophy of pragmatic commerce makes it attractive to Harper operations—agents can move through with minimal scrutiny, and the town's merchants are generally unwilling to cooperate with organizations like the Zhentarim that threaten free commerce. Paradoxically, while most of the Great Dale views Uthmere as a predatory force, other regions see it as a beacon of hope for civilization and order in the wilderness.

Campaign Integration

Uthmere serves as a friendly way station and supply hub for adventurers traveling through the region. Aldric Thorne provides a reliable Harper contact point, offering supplies, information, and safe passage when needed. The town's neutral stance and merchant focus make it an important counterpoint to military settlements like Ilmwatch, representing the values that the Harpers work to protect—freedom, fair dealing, and community prosperity without external domination.

The contrast between Uthmere's independence and Ilmwatch's Zhentarim influence illustrates larger regional tensions that may become relevant to the campaign.
