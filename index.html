Description

Small village at the head of Easting Reach fjord. Natural harbor with excellent defensive position. Recognized as a potential Harper safe haven.

The wind cuts sharp across the dark waters of the Eastern Fjord as your ship rounds the final headland. A small village clings to a narrow crescent of green between towering mountain walls and the fjord's deep waters. Towering over the village is the mist shrouded Giantspire Peak.

The Titan's Thirst - Session Outline

A creature from the cursed glacial pool threatens the valley

Session Details: Level 5-7 | Duration: 4-6 hours | Theme: Ancient curses, environmental horror, community defense

Session Overview

A massive creature, transformed by drinking from the legendary Cursed Pool of Jotunn's Tears, descends from the high peaks to threaten Fjord's End. The PCs must decide whether to defend the village, investigate the source of the curse, or attempt to cure the afflicted beast.

Warning Signs

  • Missing Wildlife: No birds, ravens have vanished
  • Nervous Animals: Village dogs whine and refuse to leave owners' sides
  • Geological Signs: Minor tremors, loose stones falling from cliffs
  • Strange Phenomena: Strange lights on high peaks three nights prior
  • Marine Anomalies: Something massive detected moving in deep fjord waters

The Threat: Titan Bear

A sound unlike anything you've ever heard echoes through the fjord‚Äîa roar that seems to shake the very foundations of the mountains. It's not the cry of any natural beast, but something deeper, more primal, touched with an anguish that makes your bones ache.

Creature Description

What was once a brown bear now stands thirty feet tall at the shoulder, its fur matted with ice and its eyes glowing with an unnatural blue light. Ancient ice clings to its massive claws, and with each breath, clouds of freezing mist pour from its muzzle.

Icelace Lake

Location & Description

A long, narrow body of water stretches across the frozen landscape‚ÄîIcelace Lake, a barrier between civilization and the Great Glacier. Its pale blue-white waters are frigid year-round, and the shores are home to the hardy Nar peoples who brave its dangers for the abundant fish and furs.
  • Location: ~100 miles north of Fjord's End; acts as a barrier between settled lands and the Great Glacier
  • Size: Approximately 150 miles long, 5-25 miles wide
  • Primary Resources: Salmon, trout, and valuable furs
  • Inhabitants: Nar peoples in semi-nomadic tribal settlements
  • Hazards: Hypothermia, predatory wildlife (bears, giant pike), thin ice, sudden storms
  • Lore: Rumored to be cursed; ancient ruins on several islands; mysterious phenomena reported

The Cursed Pool of Jotunn's Tears

‚Üí View Complete Location Details & History

Location & Description

The glacial pool stretches before you like a mirror of black ice, its surface so still it might be solid. But you can see the faint shimmer of movement beneath, and the water itself seems to pulse with a malevolent blue light. Ancient runes are carved into the surrounding stone‚Äîgiant script that predates any human settlement.
  • Location: High glacial cirque, two days' climb up the northern slopes near glacier's edge
  • Magic Type: Powerful necromantic and transmutation magic
  • Origin: Created by a noble's desperate bargain with a devil gone horribly wrong
  • Features: Ancient giant runes telling a false story; infernal runes hidden beneath
  • Effect: Transforms creatures that drink from it (monstrous enlargement with ice powers and madness)
  • Purification: Possible through high-level magic, divine intervention, or breaking the original pact

Player Response Options

Option A: Direct Defense

  • Fortify the village and prepare for siege
  • Organize evacuation of non-combatants
  • Set up defensive positions using terrain advantages
  • Organize and arm village militia

Option B: Intercept the Threat

  • Travel up the mountain to meet creature before it reaches village
  • Use terrain advantages in the mountains
  • Attempt to drive it away or kill it in favorable terrain

Option C: Investigate the Source

  • Seek out the Cursed Pool to understand what happened
  • Find a way to reverse the transformation
  • Permanently solve the problem

Option D: Diplomatic Solution

  • Attempt to communicate with the creature
  • Discover if intelligence remains beneath the curse
  • Lead creature back to pool for healing

  • Mist rising from the fjord

Rewards & Recognition

  • Offers of permanent residence or honorary citizenship
  • Access to village resources for future adventures
  • Introduction to regional contacts and allies
  • Discounted services, free lodging, equipment access

Final Note

Fjord's End represents a frontier community with deep ties to ancient magic and giant lore. Its isolated location makes it both vulnerable to supernatural threats and valuable as a safe haven. The village's response to the Titan Bear crisis will define its relationship with the PCs and determine its role in the larger campaign.
